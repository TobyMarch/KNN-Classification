<h4>
Biography
</h4>








<div
more-text="More"
will-truncate-max-height="72">








<p
class="extra">Contemporary
country
singer/songwriter
Brad
Paisley
was
born
October
28,
1972,
in
Glen
Dale,
West
Virginia;
given
his
first
guitar
at
age
eight,
he
delivered
his
first
public
performance
at
church
two
years
later.
With
his
fifty-something
guitar
teacher
Clarence
&quot;Hank&quot;
Goddard
and
two
of
the
older
man's
seasoned
musician
buddies,
the
teenaged
Paisley
formed
his
first
band,
the
C-Notes,
and
at
age
12
began
writing
his
own
material.
After
performing
in
front
of
the
local
Rotary
Club,
he
was
invited
to
appear
on
Wheeling
station
WWVA's
famed
Saturday
night
broadcast
Jamboree
USA.
Paisley's
debut
was
so
well
received
that
he
was
invited
to
join
the
program
full-time,
and
in
the
years
to
follow
he
opened
for
the
likes
of
the
Judds,
Roy
Clark,
and
Little
Jimmy
Dickens.
He
later
attended
Nashville's
Belmont
University,
serving
an
internship
with
ASCAP;
the
contacts
Paisley
made
there
helped
him
land
a
songwriting
deal
with
EMI,
and
he
also
appeared
on
countless
demos.
</p>








<p
class="extra">Signing
to
Arista,
he
issued
his
debut
solo
album,
Who
Needs
Pictures,
in
1999.
The
record
produced
two
chart-topping
singles
in
&quot;He
Didn't
Have
to
Be,&quot;
an
ode
to
loving
stepfathers,
and
&quot;We
Danced,&quot;
and
also
earned
generally
positive
reviews
for
its
diversity
of
country
styles.
In
the
meantime,
Paisley
recorded
a
duet
with
Chely
Wright,
&quot;Hard
to
Be
a
Husband,
Hard
to
Be
a
Wife,&quot;
for
the
Backstage
at
the
Grand
Ole
Opry
compilation;
the
two
later
collaborated
on
several
songs
for
Wright's
Never
Love
You
Enough
album.
The
sequel
to
Paisley's
debut,
Part
II,
was
released
in
2001
and
promptly
returned
him
to
the
Top
Five
with
&quot;Two
People
Fell
in
Love.&quot;
&quot;I'm
Gonna
Miss
Her
(The
Fishin'
Song)&quot;
gave
Paisley
his
third
chart-topper,
and
&quot;Wrapped
Around&quot;
fell
one
spot
short
of
becoming
his
fourth.
&quot;I
Wish
You'd
Stay&quot;
became
the
fourth
Top
Ten
hit
from
the
record
in
early
2003.
</p>








<p
class="extra">At
the
beginning
of
August
2005,
Paisley
put
together
a
short
&quot;director's
commentary&quot;
preview
of
his
next
album
for
his
fan
base
to
download.
The
full
album,
Time
Well
Wasted,
appeared
two
weeks
later
and
narrowly
missed
the
top
of
the
album
charts,
though
it
did
hit
number
one
on
the
country
charts.
In
2006
Brad
Paisley
Christmas,
a
collection
of
both
originals
and
covers,
came
out,
followed
by
5th
Gear
in
2007,
which
included
the
ubiquitous
&quot;Ticks,&quot;
a
sure
future
novelty
classic.
An
album
of
mostly
guitar
instrumentals
(Paisley's
excellent
guitar
playing
is
a
big
part
of
his
appeal),
Play,
followed
in
2008,
with
the
big
country
vocal
hit
&quot;Waitin'
on
a
Woman&quot;
added
in
as
a
&quot;bonus&quot;
track.
By
now
poised
at
the
very
top
of
the
commercial
country
world,
Paisley
released
American
Saturday
Night
in
2009.
American
Saturday
Night
was
greeted
by
his
strongest
reviews
yet
and
generated
the
hit
singles
&quot;Then,&quot;
&quot;Welcome
to
the
Future,&quot;
and
&quot;Water.&quot;
Paisley
bought
some
time
with
the
2010
release
Hits
Alive
—
a
double-disc
package
divided
into
one
live
set
and
one
collection
of
hits
—
and
then
returned
with
his
seventh
collection
of
new
songs,
This
Is
Country
Music,
in
May
2011.
</p>








<p
class="extra">&quot;Old
Alabama,&quot;
the
second
single
pulled
from
This
Is
Country
Music,
became
Paisley's
19th
number
one
single,
followed
quickly
by
his
20th
number
one,
the
Carrie
Underwood
duet
&quot;Remind
Me.&quot;
As
he
toured
the
album,
Paisley
also
busied
himself
in
a
variety
of
show
biz
cameos,
including
popping
up
on
the
Cars
2
soundtrack
and
guesting
on
South
Park.
After
the
tour
came
to
a
conclusion,
he
set
out
to
work
on
his
ninth
album,
Wheelhouse.
An
ambitious,
genre-hopping
album,
the
record
was
preceded
by
the
&quot;Southern
Comfort
Zone&quot;
single
in
the
fall
of
2012
and
&quot;Beat
This
Summer,&quot;
which
appeared
a
month
prior
to
Wheelhouse's
April
2013
release.
~
Steve
Huey
&amp;
Steve
Leggett,
Rovi</p>







</div>







</div>
