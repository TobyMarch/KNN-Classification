<p><i>City
Reading</i>
(2003)
started
as
an
idea
for
a
collaboration
with
contemporary
Italian
writer
<a
href="/wiki/Alessandro_Baricco?qsrc=3044"

Baricco</a>,
known
for
his
unusual
characterizations
and
lyrical,
poetic
style.
Baricco
contacted
Air
in
summer
2002
with
the
idea
to
marry
narration
of
texts
from
his
book
<i>City</i>
with
live
original
musical
accompaniment.
The
performance
premiered
live
in
November
2002
at
<a
href="/wiki/Rome?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Rome">Rome</a>'s
<a
href="/wiki/Teatro_Valle?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Teatro
Valle">Teatro
Valle</a>,
and
was
deemed
such
a
success
that
both
parties
resolved
to
make
a
full
studio
recording
that
resulted
in
the
album
<i>City
Reading:
Tre
Storie
Western</i>.</p>
