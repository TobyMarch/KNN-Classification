<p>Rapper
<a
href="http://theboombox.com/tags/fat-joe/">Fat
Joe</a>
melted
our
hearts
last
month
when
he
<a
href="http://theboombox.com/fat-joe-released-from-prison/">surprised
his
daughter</a>
on
Thanksgiving
Day
after
serving
three
months
of
a
four-month
prison
bid
on
tax
evasion
charges.
However,
their
reunion
was
short-lived
as
the
Bronx
rhymer
is
currently
serving
his
final
weeks
of
imprisonment
at
a
Miami
halfway
house.<span
id="more-56858"></span></p>










<p>“They
gave
me
a
pass
to
come
home
for
Thanksgiving,”
he
explained
to
<a
href="http://revolt.tv/news/fat-joe-is-a-free-man/D6CD4866-0F85-4718-B2AC-152D8EF4809B"
target="_blank">Revolt
TV</a>
in
his
first
post-prison
interview.
“It
was
only
for
a
couple
of
hours.”</p>










<p>Joey
Crack
spent
much
of
his
time
in
prison
reflecting,
working
out,
reading
and
getting
himself
healthy.
The
rapper
did
offer
one
anecdotal
story
about
his
first
day
in
the
big
house.</p>










<p>“The
first
day
I
walked
in,
I
was
in
the
building
so
when
they
put
me
up
to
the
floor
I
was
gonna
be
in,
like
in
the
movie
‘Lock
Up,’
everybody’s
watching
the
TV
and
the
thing
[is]
nobody
knew
Fat
Joe
was
walking
in,”
he
recalls.
“I
felt
everyone’s
breath
—
like
500
dudes
just
banging
on
the
wall,
it
was
a
lot.
I
had
to
be
like,
‘Yo
hold
up,
let
me
get
right!’
[Laughs.]
That
was
crazy.”
</p>










<p>Fat
Joe
will
officially
be
a
free
man
on
Christmas
Eve.
The
‘Pain’
rapper
is
looking
forward
to
2014.
He
also
promises
new
music
is
on
the
way.</p>










<p>“Well,
my
last
day
of
captivity
[will]
be
Christmas
Eve
and
I
already
got
this
joint
from
producer
StreetRunner
that’s
amazing,”
he
said.
“It’s
called
‘Free,’
so
look
for
that
and
I’m
just
spitting
about
everything
I
went
through
in
[prison]
and
the
things
I’m
looking
to
do.”
</p>










<p>“It’s
crazy,
once
I’m
able
to
record
it,
[because]
I’m
not
able
to
go
to
the
studio
yet
so,
soon
as
I’m
able
to
touch
that
studio
—
I’m
ready
to
go,”
he
adds.&nbsp;</p>










<p>Welcome
home,
Fat
Joe!</p>
