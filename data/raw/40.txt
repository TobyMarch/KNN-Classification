<h3><span
class="mw-headline"

<span

title="Edit
section:
Fevers

Mirrors

<p>In
2000,
Bright
Eyes
released
<i><a
href="/wiki/Fevers_and_Mirrors?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Fevers
and
Mirrors">Fevers
and
Mirrors</a>,</i>
with
new
instruments
such
as
the
<a
href="/wiki/Flute?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Flute">flute</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/Piano?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Piano">piano</a>,
and
<a
href="/wiki/Accordion?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Accordion">accordion</a>
introduced
into
the
song
arrangements.
After
&quot;An
Attempt
to
Tip
the
Scales&quot;,
there
is
a
mock
radio
interview
that
features
<a
href="/wiki/Todd_Fink?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Todd
Fink">Todd
Fink</a>
of
<a
href="/wiki/The_Faint?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
Faint">The
Faint</a>
doing
an
impression
of
Oberst
while
reading
a
script
that
Oberst
wrote.
In
this
interview,
the
fake
Oberst
presents
a
strange,
contradictory
explanation
of
his
attitude
towards
his
<a
href="/wiki/Music?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Music">music</a>.
It
acknowledges
criticisms
of
his
<a
href="/wiki/Lyrics?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Lyrics">lyrics</a>
as
overblown
and
insincere,
which
had
begun
to
appear
as
the
popularity
of
the
band
increased,
but
responds
by
stating
that
the
lyrics
are
meant
for
personal
interpretation.
Oberst
later
commented
that
&quot;It
was
a
way
to
make
fun
of
ourselves
because
the
record
is
such
a
downer.
I
mean,
that's
one
part
of
who
I
am
but
I
also
like
<a
href="/wiki/Laughter?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Laughter">laughing</a>.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-kittymagik_7-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-kittymagik-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
album
placed
170
on
Pitchfork
Media's
best
200
albums
of
the
decade.<sup
id="cite_ref-8"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

(magazine)">Time</a></i>

(magazine)">Blender</a></i>,
