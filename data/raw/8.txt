<h3><span

Formation
and
<i>The
All-American
Rejects</i></span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=The_All-American_Rejects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2"
title="Edit
section:
1999–2004:
Formation
and
The
All-American
Rejects">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<div
class="rellink
relarticle
mainarticle">








Main
article:









<a
href="/wiki/The_All-American_Rejects_(album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
All-American
Rejects
(album)">The
All-American
Rejects
(album)</a>







</div>








<p>The
band
formed
in
<a
href="/wiki/Stillwater,_Oklahoma?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Stillwater,
Oklahoma">Stillwater</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/Oklahoma?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>,
in
1999
while
members
<a
href="/wiki/Tyson_Ritter?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Tyson
Ritter">Tyson
Ritter</a>
and
Nick
Wheeler
were
still
in
high
school.
The
two
originally
met
in
1997
at
a
club
where
an
early
band
of
Wheeler's
was
performing.
It
was
here
that
Ritter
offered
himself
for
bass
duties.
Eventually
Ritter
and
Wheeler
decided
to
form
The
All-American
Rejects.<sup
id="cite_ref-SGtranslated_5-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-SGtranslated-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>In
2000,
still
in
high
school,
the
band
made
a
demo
simply
labeled
<i>The
All-American
Rejects</i>,
with
Jesse
Tabish
as
vocalist/guitarist,
Tyson
Ritter
as
vocalist/bassist
and
Nick
Wheeler
on
drums.
The
demo
CD
was
engineered,
mixed
and
mastered
by
Wheeler.
It
was
also
managed
by
Half-A-Cup
Entertainment.
The
demo
is
now
commonly
referred
to
as
<i>The
Blue
Disc</i>
or
<i>The
Blue
Album</i>.
The
CD
consisted
of
twelve
songs,
a
few
of
which
would
be
included
on
the
<i>Same
Girl,
New
Songs</i>
demo
made
in
2001.
Jesse
Tabish
later
split
from
the
group
and
later
became
a
member
of
the
folk
band
<a
href="/wiki/Other_Lives_(band)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Other
Lives
(band)">Other
Lives</a>.
Ritter
and
Wheeler
put
together
the
<i><a
href="/wiki/Same_Girl,_New_Songs?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Same
Girl,
New
Songs">Same
Girl,
New
Songs</a></i>
EP,
which
was
soon
sent
to
independent
<a
href="/wiki/Doghouse_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Doghouse
Records">Doghouse
Records</a>,
where
an
intern
saved
the
disc
from
the
trash,
and
revealed
it
to
the
label
owner.
The
All-American
Rejects
were
soon
signed
to
a
recording
contract.<sup
id="cite_ref-15"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>In
2001,
with
producer
Tim
O'Heir,
they
recorded
their
self-titled
debut
album,
<i><a
href="/wiki/The_All-American_Rejects_(album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
All-American
Rejects
(album)">The
All-American
Rejects</a></i>.
The
album
and
its
first
single,
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Swing,_Swing?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Swing,
Swing">Swing,
Swing</a>&quot;,
were
released
towards
the
end
of
2002.
The
band
began
looking
for
additional
members
for
a
live
act.<sup
id="cite_ref-16"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup>
Edmond,
Oklahoma
resident
Mike
Kennerty
joined
on
as
rhythm
guitarist
soon
before
the
album
was
released.
Soon
after,
the
band
was
having
problems
with
drummer
Tim
Campbell.
Campbell
was
later
replaced
by
drummer
Chris
Gaylor,
who
knew
and
played
in
another
band
with
Kennerty.<sup
id="cite_ref-17"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>The
band
was
then
signed
by
<a
href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="DreamWorks
Records">DreamWorks
Records</a>.
The
band
hit
the
road
for
eight
shows
in
January,
and
DreamWorks
issued
a
broader-scale
distribution
of
the
debut
LP.
The
album
peaked
at
No.&nbsp;25
on
the
Billboard
200.
The
single
&quot;Swing,
Swing&quot;
was
also
re-issued,
which
peaked
on
the
Billboard
Hot
100
at
No.&nbsp;60
as
well
as
No.&nbsp;8
on
the
Billboard
<a
href="/wiki/Modern_Rock_Tracks?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Modern
Rock
Tracks"
class="mw-redirect">Modern
Rock
Tracks</a>
chart.<sup
id="cite_ref-18"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup
id="cite_ref-19"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup><sup
id="cite_ref-billboard.com_20-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-billboard.com-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
single
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/The_Last_Song_(The_All-American_Rejects_song)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
Last
Song
(The
All-American
Rejects
song)">The
Last
Song</a>&quot;
was
also
released
spring
2003,
and
climbed
to
No.&nbsp;29
on
the
Billboard
<a
href="/wiki/Modern_Rock_Tracks?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Modern
Rock
Tracks"
class="mw-redirect">Modern
Rock
Tracks</a>
chart.<sup
id="cite_ref-billboard.com_20-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-billboard.com-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Early
in
2003,
the
band
went
on
tour
as
an
opening
act
for
<a
href="/wiki/CKY_(band)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="CKY
(band)">CKY</a>.
While
several
differing

have
occurred

they
were
kicked
off
the
tour
shortly
after
it
began.</p>








<p>In
Spring
2003,
The
All-American
Rejects
set
out
on
their
first
headlining
tour,
called
the
<i>Too
Bad
For
Hell

In

The
All-American
Rejects
released,
<i><a
href="/wiki/Live_from_Oklahoma..._The_Too_Bad_for_Hell_DVD!?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Live
from
Oklahoma...
The
Too
Bad
for
Hell
DVD!">Live

Oklahoma...

for

their

Gold
by
the
<a
href="/wiki/RIAA?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="RIAA"
class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a>.
During
this
time,
they
performed
on
the
<a
href="/wiki/Vans_Warped_Tour?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Vans
Warped
Tour"
class="mw-redirect">Vans
Warped
Tour</a>.
They
would
later
be
part
of
the
lineup
again
in
summer
2005.
In
November,
The
All-American
Rejects
joined
the
band
<a
href="/wiki/Motion_City_Soundtrack?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Motion
City
Soundtrack">Motion
City
Soundtrack</a>
for
six
shows
in
the
United
Kingdom,
the
first
date
on
the
16th,
and
the
last
on
the
22nd.<sup
id="cite_ref-21"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
