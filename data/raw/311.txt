world
tour,
the
band's
gruelling
final
tour
was
not
as
successful.
It
proved
to
be
too
much
for
Dire
Straits,
and
by
this
time
Mark
Knopfler
had
had
enough
of
such
massive
operations.
This
led
to
the
group's
second
and
final
break-up.
Bill
Flanagan
described
the
sequence
of
events
in
Gentleman's
Quarterly:
&quot;The
subsequent
world
tour
lasted
nearly
two
years,
made
mountains
of
money
and
drove
Dire
Straits
into
the
ground.
When
the
tour
was
over,
both
Knopfler's
marriage
and
his
band
were
gone&quot;.<sup
id="cite_ref-49"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
last
stop
on
the
tour
and
the
final
touring
concert
of
the
group
took
place
on
9
October
1992
in
<a
href="/wiki/Zaragoza?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>,
Spain.</p>








<p>After
the
end
of
the
tour,
Mark
Knopfler
expressed
a
wish
to
give
up
touring
on
a
big
scale,
and
took
some
time
out
from
the
music
business.
A
live
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/On_the_Night?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="On
the
Night">On
the
Night</a></i>
was
released
in
May
1993,
which
documented
the
tour,
again
to
very
mixed
reviews.
Nevertheless,
it
reached
the
UK
Top
5,
a
rare
achievement
for
a
live
album.<sup
id="cite_ref-Roberts_14-5"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-Roberts-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
four
track
<i><a
href="/wiki/Encores_(EP)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Encores
(EP)">Encores</a></i>
EP
was
also
released
and
rose
to
number
one
in
the
French
singles
chart.</p>








<p>Dire
Straits'
final
album,
<i><a
href="/wiki/Live_at_the_BBC_(Dire_Straits_album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

(Dire
Straits
album)">Live
at
the
BBC</a></i>,
was
a

of

recordings

the

1978–81,
which

featured
the
original
line-up
of
the
band.<sup
id="cite_ref-BIL_50-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-BIL-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup>
Released
in
June
1995,
their
third
and
final
live
album
was
a
contractual
release
to
<a
href="/wiki/Vertigo_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Vertigo
Records">Vertigo
Records</a>
(now
a
division
of
<a
href="/wiki/Mercury_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Mercury
Records">Mercury
Records</a>).<sup
id="cite_ref-BIL_50-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-BIL-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup>
At
this
time,
Mark
Knopfler
quietly
disbanded
Dire
Straits
and
prepared
to
work
on
his
first
fully-fledged
solo
album
(still
signed
to
Mercury
Records).</p>








<h3><span

<p><i>Brothers

<a

in

<a

in

on
<a
href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Warner
Bros.

<p>In
