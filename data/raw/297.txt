<h2><span
class="mw-headline"

<span

<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Before_Creedence:_1959.E2.80.931967">Before
Creedence:
1959–1967</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Creedence_Clearwater_Revival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2"
title="Edit
section:
Before
Creedence:
1959–1967">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>John
Fogerty,
Doug
Clifford,
and
Stu
Cook
(all
born
in
1945)
met
at
Portola
Junior
High
School
in
<a
href="/wiki/El_Cerrito,_California?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="El
Cerrito,
California">El
Cerrito,
California</a>.
The
trio
began
playing
instrumentals
and
&quot;juke
box
standards&quot;
together
under
the
name
<a
href="/wiki/The_Blue_Velvets?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
Blue
Velvets">The
Blue
Velvets</a>,<sup
id="cite_ref-rs30_5-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-rs30-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup>
also
backing
singer
Tom
Fogerty
at
live
gigs
and
in
the
recording
studio.
Tom
joined
the
band,
and
in
1964
they
signed
with
<a
href="/wiki/Fantasy_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Fantasy
Records">Fantasy
Records</a>,
an
independent
jazz
label
in
San
Francisco
that
had
released
<i><a
href="/wiki/Cast_Your_Fate_to_the_Wind?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Cast
Your
Fate
to
the
Wind">Cast
Your
Fate
to
the
Wind</a></i>,
a
national
hit
for
jazz
pianist
<a
href="/wiki/Vince_Guaraldi?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Vince
Guaraldi">Vince
Guaraldi.</a>
The
record's
success
was
the
subject
of
a
<a
href="/wiki/National_Educational_Television?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="National
Educational
Television">National
Educational
Television</a>
special,
which
prompted
budding
songwriter
John
Fogerty
to
contact
the
label.<sup
id="cite_ref-6"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup>
For
the
band's
first
release,
Fantasy
co-owner
Max
Weiss
renamed
the
group
<a
href="/wiki/The_Golliwogs?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
Golliwogs">the
Golliwogs</a>
(after
the
children's
literary
character,
<a
href="/wiki/Golliwogg?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Golliwogg">Golliwogg</a>),
apparently
because
a
wave
of
popular
British
bands
had
similar
names.<sup
class="Template-Fact"
style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a
href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed"
target="_blank"
title="Wikipedia:Citation
needed"><span
title="This
claim
needs
references
to
reliable
sources.
(September
2011)">citation
needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>








<p>Band
roles
changed
during
this
period.
Stu
Cook
switched
from
piano
to
bass
guitar
and
Tom
Fogerty
from
lead
vocals
to
rhythm
guitar;
John
became
the
band's
lead
vocalist
and
primary
songwriter.
In
Tom
Fogerty's
words:
&quot;I
could
sing,
but
John
had
a
sound!&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-7"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Early_success:_1967.E2.80.9368">Early
success:
1967–68</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Creedence_Clearwater_Revival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3"
title="Edit
section:
Early
success:
1967–68">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>In
1966,
the
group
suffered
a
setback
when
John
Fogerty
and
Doug
Clifford
were
drafted
into
military
service.
Fogerty
enlisted
in
the
<a
href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="United
States
Army
Reserve">Army
Reserve</a>
and
Clifford
in
the
<a
href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard_Reserve?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="United
States
Coast
Guard
Reserve">United
States
Coast
Guard
Reserve</a>.</p>








<p>In
1967,
<a
href="/wiki/Saul_Zaentz?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Saul
Zaentz">Saul
Zaentz</a>
bought
Fantasy
Records
and
offered
the
band
a
chance
to
record
a
full-length
album
on
the
condition
that
they
change
their
name.
Never
having
liked
&quot;the
Golliwogs&quot;,
the
four
readily
agreed.
Zaentz
and
the
band
agreed
to
come
up
with
ten
suggestions
each,
but
he
enthusiastically
agreed
to
their
first:
Creedence
Clearwater
Revival
(CCR).
The
name's
three
elements
come
from
the
following
sources:</p>








<ul>









<li>Tom
Fogerty's
friend
Credence
Newball,
whose
name
they
changed
to
form
the
word
<i>creed</i>
(as
in
<a
href="/wiki/Creed?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Creed">creed</a>);</li>









<li>a
television
commercial
for
<a
href="/wiki/Olympia_Brewing_Company?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Olympia
Brewing
Company">Olympia
beer</a>
(&quot;clear
water&quot;);
and</li>









<li>the
four
members'
renewed
commitment
to
their
band.</li>








</ul>








<p>Rejected
contenders
for
the
band's
name
included
Muddy
Rabbit,
Gossamer
Wump,
and
Creedence
Nuball
and
the
Ruby,
but
the
last
was
the
start
that
led
to
their
finalized
name.<sup
id="cite_ref-isbn_8-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-isbn-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>By
1968,
Fogerty
and
Clifford
had
been
discharged
from
military
service,
and
all
four
members
had
quit
their
jobs
to
begin
an
intense
schedule
of
rehearsing
and
playing
full-time
at
clubs.<sup
class="Template-Fact"
style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a

needed"><span

<a

title="AM
radio"
class="mw-redirect">AM
radio</a>
programmers
around
the
U.S.A.
took
note
when
a
song
from
the
album,
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Suzie_Q_(song)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Suzie
Q
(song)"
class="mw-redirect">Suzie
Q</a>&quot;,
received
substantial
airplay
in
the
San
Francisco
Bay
Area
and
on
Chicago's
<a
href="/wiki/WLS_(AM)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="WLS
(AM)">WLS</a>.<sup
class="Template-Fact"
style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a
href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed"
target="_blank"
title="Wikipedia:Citation
needed"><span
title="This
claim
needs
references
to
reliable
sources.
(August
2011)">citation
needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
A

<a
href="/wiki/Rockabilly?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Rockabilly">rockabilly</a>
singer
<a
href="/wiki/Dale_Hawkins?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

&quot;Suzie

<a
href="/wiki/Top_40?qsrc=3044"

<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Peak_success:_1969.E2.80.9370">Peak
success:
1969–70</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Creedence_Clearwater_Revival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4"
title="Edit
section:
Peak
success:
1969–70">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>After
their
breakthrough,
CCR
began
touring
and
started
work
on
their
second
album,
<i><a
href="/wiki/Bayou_Country?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

Country">Bayou
Country</a></i>
(1969),
at
<a
href="/wiki/RCA?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="RCA">RCA</a>
Studios
in
<a
href="/wiki/Los_Angeles?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Los
Angeles">Los
Angeles</a>.
A
No.&nbsp;7
platinum
hit,
the
record
was
their
first
in
a
string
of
hit
albums
and
singles
that
continued
uninterrupted
for
three
years.
The
single
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Proud_Mary?qsrc=3044"

Mary">Proud

&quot;Born
on

No.&nbsp;2
on

national
<i><a
href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

magazine"
class="mw-redirect">Billboard</a></i>
chart.
The
former
would
eventually
become
the
group's
most-covered
song,
with
some
100
cover
versions
by
other
artists
to
date,
including
a
hit
version
in
1971
by
<a
href="/wiki/Ike_%26_Tina_Turner?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Ike
&amp;
Tina
Turner">Ike
&amp;
Tina
Turner</a>.
John
Fogerty
cites
this
song
as
being
the
result
of
high
spirits
on
gaining
his
discharge
from
the
Army
Reserve.<sup
class="Template-Fact"
style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a
href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed"
target="_blank"
title="Wikipedia:Citation
needed"><span
title="This
claim
needs
references
to
reliable
sources.
(August
2011)">citation
needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
The

also

a

of
the
rock
&amp;
roll
classic
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Good_Golly_Miss_Molly?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Good
Golly
Miss
Molly">Good
Golly
Miss
Molly</a>&quot;
and
the
band's
nine-minute
live-show

&quot;Keep
On
Chooglin'&quot;.</p>








<p>Weeks
later,

1969,
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Bad_Moon_Rising_(song)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

Moon
Rising
(song)">Bad
Moon
Rising</a>&quot;
backed

&quot;<a

(Creedence

Revival

was

and

at

The

<i><a

(album)">Green
River</a>,</i>

in
August

&quot;Green

No.&nbsp;2
on
the

The

of
&quot;Green
River,&quot;
&quot;Commotion&quot;,
peaked
at
No.&nbsp;30
and

&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Night_Time_Is_the_Right_Time?qsrc=3044"

Right

<p>CCR

<a

&quot;The

to

class="reference"><a
