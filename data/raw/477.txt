<p><b>Gary
Allan
Herzberg</b>
was
born
and
raised
in
<a
href="/wiki/La_Mirada,_California?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="La
Mirada,
California">La
Mirada,
California</a>,<sup
id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-allmusic-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>
to
Harley
and
Mary
Herzberg.<sup
id="cite_ref-young_2-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-young-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup>
To
ensure
that
the
family
would
focus
on
music,
Allan's
mother
insisted
that
the
family's
guitars
would
always
remain
visible
in
the
home.
At
age
thirteen,
Allan
began
playing
in
<a
href="/wiki/Honky_tonks?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Honky
tonks"
class="mw-redirect">honky
tonks</a>
with
his
father.<sup
id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-2"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-allmusic-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup
id="cite_ref-paulik_3-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-paulik-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>
Two
years
later,
he
was
offered
his
first
recording
contract,
from
<a
href="/wiki/A%26M_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="A&amp;M
Records">A&amp;M
Records</a>,
but
rejected
the
deal.
His
parents
wanted
him
to
finish
his
education
and
his
father
felt
that
Allan
had
yet
to
develop
his
own
distinctive
style.<sup
id="cite_ref-dave_4-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-dave-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
Despite
his
commitment
to
finishing
school,
Allan
reflects
that
he
was
rarely
alert
in
class.
&quot;I
played
the
bars
at
night,
I
was
half
asleep
when
I
got
to
school.
I
thought
sleep
was
what
you
did
when
you
go
to
school.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-dave_4-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-dave-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>After
graduating
from
<a
href="/wiki/La_Serna_High_School?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="La
Serna
High
School">La
Serna
High
School</a>
in
<a
href="/wiki/Whittier,_California?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Whittier,
California">Whittier,
California</a>,
Allan
continued
to
play
in
the
bars
with
his
band,
the
Honky
Tonk
Wranglers.
Many
of
the
venues
they
played
were
packed,
and
promoters
often
tried
to
move
them
to
larger
clubs.
The
moves
would
have
required
him
to
stop
playing
some
of
the
older
country
music,
such
as
covers
of
<a
href="/wiki/George_Jones?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="George
Jones">George
Jones</a>
songs,
so
Allan
refused.<sup
id="cite_ref-paulik_3-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-paulik-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>In
1987,
Gary
married
his
first
wife,
Tracy
Taylor.
They
since
divorced.
He
married
model
Danette
Day
on
November
28,
1998
in
South
Carolina
and
they

in

5,
2001),
committed
<a
href="/wiki/Suicide?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Suicide">suicide</a>
on
October
25,
2004.<sup
id="cite_ref-garyallan575_6-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-garyallan575-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup

<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Nashville_connection">Nashville
connection</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Gary_Allan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3"
title="Edit
section:
Nashville
connection">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>Allan
was
introduced
to
songwriter/producer
<a
href="/wiki/Byron_Hill?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Byron
Hill">Byron
Hill</a>
on
August
28,
1993
by
a
mutual
friend
and
talent-scout
Jim
Seal,
at
a
bar
called
the
Lion
D'or
in
<a
href="/wiki/Downey,_California?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Downey,
California">Downey,
California</a>,
where
Allan
was
already
regularly
performing.
Seal
and
Hill
had
asked
Allan
if
they
could
showcase
an
unsigned
act
that
they
were
developing
there.
Hill
had
arranged
to
bring
the
head
of
<a
href="/wiki/A%26R?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="A&amp;R"
class="mw-redirect">A&amp;R</a>
from
a
major
label
to
the
show
to
see
this
other
act
perform.
Allan
kindly
let
them
use
his
stage
for
the
event,
giving
the
new
act
the
opening
performance
slot
that
night.
Hill
promised
Gary
that
they
would
make
sure
the
A&amp;R
person
remained
there
to
see
his
portion
of
the
show.
Everyone
was
knocked
out
with
Allan's
performance,
and
very
impressed
with
his
voice.
From
that
point
on,
Byron
Hill
began
sending
Gary
songs.
Without
any
serious
funding
at
the
time,
Hill
arranged
for
Allan
to
go
into
Seal's
small
studio
in
California
to
try
his
vocals
on
some
of
existing
demo
tracks
that
Byron
had
sent
to
Gary
from
<a
href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Nashville,
Tennessee">Nashville,
Tennessee</a>.
Meanwhile,
Hill
became
head
of
A&amp;R
at
<a
href="/wiki/BNA_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="BNA
Records">BNA
Entertainment</a>
on
October
29
of
that
same
year
and
immediately
wanted
to
sign
Allan
to
BNA,
but
the
then
current
roster
conditions
and
other
circumstances
related
to
the
planned
restructuring
of
<a
href="/wiki/RCA_Nashville?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="RCA
Nashville"
class="mw-redirect">RCA/BNA
Nashville</a>
stood
in
the
way.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="From_demo_to_deal">From
demo
to
deal</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Gary_Allan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4"
title="Edit
section:
From
demo
to
deal">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>In
the
meantime,
Allan
took
a
job
selling
cars.
He
left
a
demo
tape
in
the
glove
box
of
a
truck
purchased
by
a
wealthy
couple.
When
the
couple
discovered
that
he
was
the
singer,
they
wrote
him
a
check
for
$12,000.<sup
id="cite_ref-dave_4-2"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-dave-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
This
independent
funding
allowed
Allan
to
go
to
Nashville
to
record
some
of
the
songs
that
were
on
that
early
demo
tape
with
<a
href="/wiki/Byron_Hill?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Byron
Hill">Byron
Hill</a>
as
producer.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>
On
September
11,
1995,
they
worked
at
Javelina
Studios
for
a
couple
of
days
on
the
four
songs
that
Hill
immediately
showed
to
labels.
Allan's
recordings
brought
serious
responses
from
several
labels
including
<a
href="/wiki/Mercury_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Mercury
Records">Mercury</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/RCA_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="RCA
Records">RCA</a>,
and
<a
href="/wiki/Decca_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Decca
Records">Decca</a>.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-2"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>
A
meeting
was
then
held
at
a
Nashville
hotel
among
Hill,
Allan,
and
friend
of
Allan's,
who
was
a
program
director
for
a
radio
station
in
California.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-3"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>The
meeting
was
to
arrange
two
showcases
in
<a
href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Los
Angeles,
California"
class="mw-redirect">Los
Angeles,
California</a>,
to
put
Allan
on
stage
at
two
of
the
radio
station's
regular
nights
at
a
local
club.
Byron
arranged
for
staffers
at
the
Nashville
office
of
Decca
Records
to
attend
the
first
showcase
held
on
November
1,
1995.
Decca
immediately
wanted
to
sign
Allan,
and
knowing
that
Byron
was
lining
up
other
labels
to
see
Gary,
Decca
asked
them
to
cancel
the
second
showcase.
A
rep
from
RCA
was
already
booked
to
see
the
second
showcase
the
following
week,
but
the
&quot;bird-in-hand&quot;
deal
offer
was
too
tempting
for
both
Byron
and
Gary,
so
they
committed
to
the
Decca
offer.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-4"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>
Decca
staffer
Mark
Wright
and
Byron
Hill
co-produced
Gary's
first
three
albums
for
Decca
beginning
sessions
on
March
11,
1996
for
<i>Used
Heart
for
Sale</i>,
then
<i>It
Would
Be
You</i>,
both
of
which
yielded
top
five
singles,
and
later
<i>Smoke
Rings
in
the
Dark</i>
(which
also
included
Tony
Brown
as
a
co-producer).
It
was
during
the
recording
of
the
first
album
that
they
recorded
&quot;It
Must
Have
Been
Ol'
Santa
Claus&quot;,
as
an
added
track
to
be
packaged
on
various
MCA/Decca
Christmas
compilations.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-5"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>
Then
Byron
and
Gary
got
a
personal
call
from
<a
href="/wiki/Harry_Connick,_Jr.?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Harry
Connick,
Jr.">Harry
Connick,
Jr.</a>,
the
writer
of
the
song,
thanking
them
for
the
recording,
during
which
he
added
a
few
of
his
<a
href="/wiki/Dixieland?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Dixieland">New
Orleans
Jazz</a>
style
&quot;very
cool
man!&quot;
compliments.
The
Christmas
recording
has
been
since
released
on
at
least
four
compilations.
The
merger
of
<a
href="/wiki/Polygram?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Polygram"
class="mw-redirect">Polygram</a>,
Decca,
and
<a
href="/wiki/MCA_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="MCA
Records">MCA
Records</a>
marked
the
closing
of
Decca
and
Gary
was
moved
to
MCA
Records.<sup
id="cite_ref-hill_9-6"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hill-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="It_Would_Be_You"><i>It
Would
Be
You</i></span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Gary_Allan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7"
title="Edit
section:
It
Would
Be
You">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>His
second
album,
<i><a
href="/wiki/It_Would_Be_You?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="It
Would
Be
You">It
Would
Be
You</a></i>,
was
released
in
1998.
Although
Allan
had
been
writing
songs
since
he
was
a
teenager,<sup
id="cite_ref-dave_4-3"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-dave-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
he
does
not
hesitate
to
bump
his
work
from
his
albums
in
favor
of
those
written
by
other
songwriters
he
respects.
For
his
second
album,
Allan
replaced
one
of
his
songs
with
&quot;No
Judgement
Day&quot;,
written
by
Allen
Shamblin.
Although
the
song
was
a
hidden
acoustic
track,
radio
stations
began
giving
it
heavy
airplay.<sup
id="cite_ref-dave_4-4"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-dave-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
song
tells
the
story
of
a
restaurant
owner
from
a
small
town
in
Texas,
where
ex
co-workers
killed
him
in
search
of
money,
for
drugs
and
alcohol.
The
<a
href="/wiki/It_Would_Be_You_(Gary_Allan_song)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="It
Would
Be
You
(Gary
Allan
song)"
class="mw-redirect">title
track</a>
of
the

<h2><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Sound">Sound</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Gary_Allan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17"
title="Edit
section:
Sound">edit</a>]</span></h2>








<p>Allan's
voice
is
described
as
&quot;raspy
and
unpolished.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-paulik_3-3"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-paulik-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
<i>New
York
Times</i>
describes
his
music
as
&quot;elegant,
often
deadpan
songs
[that]
tend
toward
manly
understatement.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-sanneh_22-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-sanneh-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup>
His
sound
is
heavily
influenced
by
the
<a
href="/wiki/Bakersfield_Sound?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Bakersfield
Sound"
class="mw-redirect">Bakersfield</a>
scene,
especially
<a
href="/wiki/Buck_Owens?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Buck
Owens">Buck
Owens</a>
and
<a
href="/wiki/Merle_Haggard?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Merle
Haggard">Merle
Haggard</a>.
He
prefers
this
sound
to
that
of
the
more
pop
country
that
is
prevalent
on
country
radio,
because
&quot;the
songs
have
got
to
have
soul,
have
real
meaning....Country
music
is...what
happens
during
the
week.
Rock
'n
roll
is
about
what
happens
at
the
weekend.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-dave_4-7"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-dave-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
Because
his
sound
is
different
from
many
of
the
current
crop
of
country
singers,
Allan
has
at
times
had
difficulty
getting
radio
to
play
his
singles.
He
says
he
has
to
&quot;walk
a
real
fine
line&quot;
to
&quot;make
sure
that
I
get
traditional
stuff
on
the
radio.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-paulik_3-4"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-paulik-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
