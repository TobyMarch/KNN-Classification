<p

Allman,

Circus

(AP

</div>










<p>Gregg
Allman
-
a
survivor
of
drug
and
alcohol
abuse,
a
liver
transplant
and
six
marriages
-
has
plenty
to
be
happy
about
these
days.
For
starters,
his
Gregg
Allman
Band
homecoming
show
Jan.
8
at
the
Sailor
Circus
Arena
<a
href="http://www.ticketsarasota.com/2013/12/23/gregg-allman-show-in-sarasota-is-sold-out/">sold
out
two
weeks
in
advance</a>.
Then,
a
couple
days
after
playing
Sarasota,
the
66-year-old
singer
who
got
sober
nearly
two
decades
ago,
will
be
honored
with
the
all-star
tribute
concert
called
“All
My
Friends:
Celebrating
the
Songs
&amp;
Voice
of
Gregg
Allman.”
Next
up?
A
movie
based
on
Allman’s
bestselling
memoir
begins
filming.</p>










<p>So,
yeah,
we
had
a
bunch
of
stuff
to
discuss
when
the
singer,
songwriter,
keyboardist
and
guitarist
best
known
as
the
leader
of
the
Allman
Brothers
Band
and
composer
of
such
classics
as
“Midnight
Rider,”
“Melissa”
and
“Whipping
Post”
called
from
his
home
near
Savannah,
Ga.
He
even
talked
about
the
possibility
of
performing
with
current
Sarasotan
Dickey
Betts.
It’s
a
sticky
subject.
Gregg
and
the
rest
of
the
founding
Allman
Brothers
Band
members
fired
Betts
in
2000.</p>










<p><strong>The
last
time
you
performed
here,
at
the
2006
Sarasota
Blues
Fest,
it
was
a
really
special
show.
How
do
you
feel
about
returning
to
your
old
hometown?</strong></p>










<p>It
will
be
good
to
be
back
in
Sarasota
and
see
all
the
great
fans.
I
haven’t
played
there
in
a
long
time
and
I’m
happy
to
return.</p>










<p><strong>Do
you
have
a
favorite
song
you
wrote
while
living
here?</strong></p>










<p>I
wrote
a
lot
of
stuff
around
there,
on
Anna
Maria
Island,
sitting
on
my
dock.
I
wrote
most
of
the
stuff
from
“I’m
No
Angel”
(1986)
and
“Just
Before
the
Bullets
Fly”
(1988)
when
I
lived
on
Bradenton
Beach.</p>










<p><strong>Who
are
you
most
excited
about
hearing
perform
your
songs
at
the
Jan.
10
tribute
show
in
Atlanta?</strong></p>










<p>Man,
there
are
so
many
people
on
that
show
I
haven’t
seen
in
so
long,
especially
Jackson
(Browne).
We
used
to
be
roommates,
not
really,
but
we
hung
together
so
much
we
might’ve
been
roommates.
This
was
way
before
either
of
us
were
known.
We
were
broke
as
we
could
be.
But
I’m
excited
about
everybody.
Taj
Mahal,
Keb’
Mo’,
all
of
them;
Natalie
Cole,
she’s
a
good
friend
of
mine.</p>










<p><strong>I
read
you
are
returning
to
the
studio
with
T-Bone
Burnett
(producer
of
Allman’s
acclaimed
2011
solo
album
“Low
Country
Blues,”
a
collection
of
mostly
old
blues
songs).
What
can
you
tell
me
about
the
material
you
will
be
recording?</strong></p>










<p>It’s
kind
of
a
second
half
of
the
last
one
but
we’ve
postponed
recording
to
May,
just
so
much
stuff
going
on.
There
will
be
classic
blues
material
and
stuff
I
wrote
by
myself.
I
got
to
road
test
them.
You’ll
hear
them
in
Sarasota.</p>










<p
style="text-align:
center;"><strong><a
href="http://www.ticketsarasota.com/slideshow/gregg-allman-discography/">Gregg
Allman
discography:
a
look
at
his
seven
solo
albums
</a></strong></p>










<p><strong>In
recent
years,
your
Gregg
Allman
Band
shows
have
included
killer
performances
of
Bob
Dylan’s
“Just
Like
a
Woman,”
Wilson
Pickett’s
“I
Found
a
Love”
and
your
guitarist
Scott
Sharrard’s
rocker
“Love
like
Kerosene.”
Any
of
those
songs
appearing
on
the
upcoming
album?</strong></p>










<p>They
just
might.</p>










<p><strong>But
you’re
not
going
to
tell
me
because
you
want
it
to
be
a
surprise.</strong></p>










<div
id="attachment_408784"
class="wp-caption
alignleft"
style="width:
346px">











<img
itemprop="image"
class="
wp-image-408784
"

Allman

/>











<p
class="wp-caption-text">Rock

Gregg
Allman

<p>That’s
a
fact.
Wouldn’t
be
a
surprise.</p>










<p><strong>Do
you
plan
on
being
on
set
when
“Midnight
Rider:
The

Allman
Story”
starts
shooting
later
this

<p>I’ll
be
in
and
out.
I’m
on
tour
in
January
until
the
20th
but
then
I
should
be
there.</p>










<p><strong>As
an
executive
producer
of
the
movie,
what’s
the
most
important
thing
you
want
to
see
the
director
and
actors
get
right?</strong></p>










<p>(Laughs)
The
facts.</p>










<p><strong>Any
in
particular?</strong></p>










<p>No.
Just
all
of
them.
This
is
all
very
new
to
me.
I
can’t
say
much
about
the
film.
I’m
still
in
a
crash
course
learning
about
all
this.</p>










<p><strong><a
href="http://www.heraldtribune.com/article/20130309/ARTICLE/130309580">At
the

for
Dan
Toler</a>
(guitarist
in
the
Allman
Brothers
Band
and
Gregg
Allman
Band)
there
were
all
these
beautiful
photos
being
projected
of
you
with
him
and
his
brother,
Frankie
Toler
(drummer
in
the
Allman
Brothers
Band
and
Gregg
Allman
Band).
You
made
some
great
music
with
those
guys
while
living
down
here.
How
would
you
like
people
to
remember
the
Toler
brothers?</strong></p>










<p>Wonderful
people
and
very
talented
and
I
miss
them
so.</p>










<p><strong><a
href="http://www.ticketsarasota.com/2013/09/23/dickey-betts-reunites-with-derek-trucks-on-allman-brothers-band-classics/">Dickey
Betts
recently
sat
in
with
Derek
Trucks</a>
(current
Allman
Brothers
Band
guitarist)
and
Susan
Tedeschi’s
band.
What
would
you
say
if
Dickey
called
you
up
and
asked
to
sit
in
with
you
at
your
show
in
Sarasota?</strong></p>










<p>I
would
tell
him
it
would
be
fine.</p>










<p><strong>Rumor
has
it
your
old
pal
<a
href="http://www.ticketsarasota.com/2013/03/11/leonard-cohen-returns-to-tampa-with-ex-sarasotan-neil-larsen/">Neil
Larsen
(keyboardist
who
recently
toured
and
recorded
with
Leonard
Cohen)</a>
is
going
to
be
in
Sarasota
at
the

time
as
your
show.
Is
there
any
chance
of
him
sitting
in
with
you?</strong></p>










<p>I’m
sure
there
is
if
he’s
there.</p>










<p><strong>We’ve
been
waiting
a
decade
now,
when
might
we
expect
a
new
Allman
Brothers
Band
studio
album?</strong></p>










<p>Not
yet.</p>










<p><strong>One
of
my
favorite
Duane
Allman
performances
is
the
Allman
Brothers
Band’s
show
at
The
Warehouse
in
New
Orleans
from
September
of
1971.
Any
chance
of
that
getting
an
official
release?</strong></p>

<p

</div>










<p>I
don’t
know.
You

of

a

as

to.</p>










<p><strong>How
about
a

album
from

“Gregg

<p>There

next

some

11

When

get

for
a

<p><strong>There

out
there
of

Allman
Band
ready

<p>I

a

<p><strong>Another
album
with

a
