<h2
class="rm">Biography</h2>










<h3
class="remove-top-margin"><span
class="locality">Hertfordshire</span>,
<span
class="country-name">United
Kingdom</span>
(<span
itemprop="foundingDate">1968</span>
–
1976,
1984
–
present)</h3>









</hgroup>

<div
class="wiki">









<div
id="wikiAbstract"
class="wiki-abstract"
data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











Deep
Purple
is
an
English











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">rock</a>
band
formed
in
Hertfordshire
in
1968.
Together
with











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Led+Zeppelin"
class="bbcode_artist">Led
Zeppelin</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Black+Sabbath"
class="bbcode_artist">Black
Sabbath</a>
they
are
considered
to
be
heavy
metal
pioneers.
Especially
influential
to
heavy
metal
were











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Ian+Gillan"
class="bbcode_artist">Ian
Gillan</a>’s
powerful
screams
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Ritchie+Blackmore"
class="bbcode_artist">Ritchie
Blackmore</a>’s
virtuoso
solos.
Deep
Purple
were
also
very
influential
to











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/progressive%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">progressive
rock</a>.










<br
/>











<br
/>
Deep
Purple’s
early
output
ranged
from
rock
(such
as
their
cover
of











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Joe+South"
class="bbcode_artist">Joe
South</a>’s
“










<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:0NcpDHxwyI39iZhhuMya4j"></span>










<a
title="Deep
Purple
–
Hush"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/_/Hush"
class="bbcode_track">Hush</a>)”
to
strongly
classically
influenced
pieces
(such
as
“










<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:2EgKboJ85SyP3geuEZrSYt"></span>










<a
title="Deep
Purple
–
April"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/_/April"
class="bbcode_track">April</a>”,
from
their
third
album).
Their
first
few
albums
contained
very
long
solos,
such
as
those
on
the
band’s
covers
of
“










<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:4PbJDqTua2dK8ZH1brc3jI"></span>










<a
title="Deep
Purple
–
Hey
Joe"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/_/Hey+Joe"
class="bbcode_track">Hey
Joe</a>”
and
“










<a
title="Deep
Purple
–
I'm
So
Glad"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/_/I%27m+So+Glad"
class="bbcode_track">I’m
So
Glad</a>”.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
After
their
third
album,
founding
member
and
guitarist
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Ritchie+Blackmore"
class="bbcode_artist">Ritchie
Blackmore</a>
in
particular
felt
the
band
should
move
in
a
harder
direction
-
and
felt
that
then
singer
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Rod+Evans"
class="bbcode_artist">Rod
Evans</a>
and
bassist
<span
title="Unknown
artist"
class="bbcode_unknown">Nicky
Simper</span>
were
incapable
of
working
in
that
direction.
Both
were
therefore
let
go,
and
replaced
with
singer
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Ian+Gillan"
class="bbcode_artist">Ian
Gillan</a>
and
bassist
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Roger+Glover"
class="bbcode_artist">Roger
Glover</a>
from
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Episode+Six"
class="bbcode_artist">Episode
Six</a>,
producing
what
is
now
thought
of
as
the
classic
Deep
Purple
lineup
with
keyboardist
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jon+Lord"
class="bbcode_artist">Jon
Lord</a>
and
drummer
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Ian+Paice"
class="bbcode_artist">Ian
Paice</a>.<br
/>
<br
/>
The
first
output
of
this
new
lineup
was
a
mixed
electric
and
orchestral
album
with
the
London
Philharmonic
Orchestra,
<a
title="Deep
Purple
-
Deep
Purple
In
Concert"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/Deep+Purple+In+Concert"
class="bbcode_album">Deep
Purple
In
Concert</a>,
with
the
centrepiece
being
Lord’s
“<a
title="Deep
Purple
–
Concerto
For
Group
And
Orchestra"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/_/Concerto+For+Group+And+Orchestra"
class="bbcode_track">Concerto
For
Group
And
Orchestra</a>”.
The
whole
project
was
reputedly
initiated
after
idle
chat
with
the
band’s
manager
about
the
possibility
led
to
him
book
the
orchestra
and
give
the
inexperienced
composer
a
deadline
to
produce
the
work
of
a
public
concert.<br
/>
<br
/>
Following
on
from
this
unusual
venture
were
four
very
influential
studio
albums
over
the
next
four
years:
<a
title="Deep
Purple
-
Deep
Purple
In
Rock"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/Deep+Purple+In+Rock"
class="bbcode_album">Deep
Purple
In
Rock</a>,
<a
title="Deep
Purple
-
Fireball"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/Fireball"
class="bbcode_album">Fireball</a>,
<a
title="Deep
Purple
-
Machine
Head"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/Machine+Head"
class="bbcode_album">Machine
Head</a>,
and
<a
title="Deep
Purple
-
Who
Do
We
Think
We
Are"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/Who+Do+We+Think+We+Are"
class="bbcode_album">Who
Do
We
Think
We
Are</a>?,
and
the
live
album
<a
title="Deep
Purple
-
Made
In
Japan"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple/Made+In+Japan"
class="bbcode_album">Made
In
Japan</a>.
</span>
