<h2
class="rm">Biography</h2>










<h3
class="remove-top-margin"><span
class="locality">Newcastle</span>,
<span
class="country-name">United
Kingdom</span>
(<span

–

</hgroup>








</header>








<div
class="wiki">









<div
id="wikiAbstract"
class="wiki-abstract"
data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











Dire
Straits
were
a











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/british"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">british</a>











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">rock</a>
band
from
Newcastle,
United
Kingdom,
formed
in
1977
by











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Mark+Knopfler"
class="bbcode_artist">Mark
Knopfler</a>
(guitar
and
vocals),
his
brother











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/David+Knopfler"
class="bbcode_artist">David
Knopfler</a>
(guitar),











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/John+Illsley"
class="bbcode_artist">John
Illsley</a>
(bass),
and











<span
title="Unknown
artist"
class="bbcode_unknown">Pick
Withers</span>
(drums),
and
subsequently
managed
by
Ed
Bicknell.
Dire
Straits
emerged
during
the











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/post-punk"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">post-punk</a>
era
of
the
late
’70s,
and
while
their
sound
was
minimalistic
and
stripped
down,
they
owed
little
to











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/punk"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">punk</a>.











<span
class="hide">
If
anything,
the
band
was
a
direct
outgrowth
of
the
roots
revivalism
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/pub%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">pub
rock</a>,
but
where
pub
rock
celebrated
good
times,
Dire
Straits
were
melancholy.
Led
by
guitarist/vocalist
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Mark+Knopfler"
class="bbcode_artist">Mark
Knopfler</a>,
the
group
built
their
sound
upon
the
laid-back
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/blues-rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">blues-rock</a>
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/J.J.+Cale"
class="bbcode_artist">J.J.
Cale</a>,
but
they
also
had
jazz
and
country
inflections,
occasionally
dipping
into
the
epic
song
structures
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/progressive%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">progressive
rock</a>.
The
band’s
music
was
offset
by
Knopfler’s
lyrics,
which
approximated
the
winding,
stream-of-conscious
narratives
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bob+Dylan"
class="bbcode_artist">Bob
Dylan</a>.
As
their
career
progressed,
Dire
Straits
became
more
refined
and
their
new
maturity
happened
to
coincide
with
the
rise
of
MTV
and
the
compact
disc.
These
two
musical
revolutions
from
the
mid-’80s
helped
make
Dire
Straits’
fifth
studio
album,
Brothers
in
Arms,
an
international
blockbuster.
The
band
-
along
with
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Eric+Clapton"
class="bbcode_artist">Eric
Clapton</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Phil+Collins"
class="bbcode_artist">Phil
Collins</a>,
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Steve+Winwood"
class="bbcode_artist">Steve
Winwood</a>
-
became
one
of
the
leaders
of
a
group
of
self-consciously
mature
veteran
rock
&amp;
rollers
in
the
late
’80s
that
designed
their
music
to
appeal
to
aging
baby
boomers.
</span>
