<h3

<div

<div
id="wikiAbstract"

<div
class="wiki-text">











Thin
Lizzy
are
an
Irish
hard
rock
band
who
formed
in
Dublin,
Ireland
in
1969.
The
band
were
led
throughout
their
recording
career
by
bassist,
songwriter
and
singer











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Phil+Lynott"
class="bbcode_artist">Phil
Lynott</a>,
and
are
best
known
for
their
songs











<a
title="Thin
Lizzy
–
Whiskey
in
the
Jar"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Thin+Lizzy/_/Whiskey+in+the+Jar"
class="bbcode_track">Whiskey
in
the
Jar</a>,











<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:0Kmzc0wdoh1JCYnORa1luW"></span>










<a
title="Thin
Lizzy
–
Jailbreak"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Thin+Lizzy/_/Jailbreak"
class="bbcode_track">Jailbreak</a>
and











<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:0XoFIIsH5zjBlgaKlKFFRS"></span>










<a
title="Thin
Lizzy
–
The
Boys
Are
Back
in
Town"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Thin+Lizzy/_/The+Boys+Are+Back+in+Town"
class="bbcode_track">The
Boys
Are
Back
in
Town</a>,
all
major
international
hits
still
played
regularly
on











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/hard%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">hard
rock</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/classic%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">classic
rock</a>
radio
stations.
After
Lynott
died,
various
incarnations
of
the
band
have
emerged
over
the
years
culminating
in
a
more
stable
line-up
based
around
guitarists











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Scott+Gorham"
class="bbcode_artist">Scott
Gorham</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/John+Sykes"
class="bbcode_artist">John
Sykes</a>.
Sykes
left
the
band
in
June
2009.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
Thin
Lizzy’s
leader,
Lynott
was
composer
or
co-composer
of
almost
all
their
songs.
He
was
also
one
of
the
few
black
men
to
achieve
significant
success
in
hard
rock.
As
well
as
being
multiracial,
the
band
drew
its
members
not
only
from
both
sides
of
the
Irish
border
but
also
from
both
the
Catholic
and
Protestant
communities.
Their
music
reflects
a
wide
range
of
influences,
including
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/country%20music"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">country
music</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/psychedelic%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">psychedelic
rock</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/irish"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">irish</a>
music
and
traditional
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/irish%20folk"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">irish
folk</a>
music,
but
is
generally
classified
as
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/hard%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">hard
rock</a>
or
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/heavy%20metal"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">heavy
metal</a>.<br
/>
<br
/>
Critic
for
allmusic
John
Dougan
wrote:
“As
the
band’s
creative
force,
Lynott
was
a
more
insightful
and
intelligent
writer
than
many
of
his
ilk,
preferring
slice-of-life
working-class
dramas
of
love
and
hate
influenced
by
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bob+Dylan"
class="bbcode_artist">Bob
Dylan</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Morrison"
class="bbcode_artist">Van
Morrison</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bruce+Springsteen"
class="bbcode_artist">Bruce
Springsteen</a>,
and
virtually
all
of
the
Irish
literary
tradition.”
Van
Morrison,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jeff+Beck"
class="bbcode_artist">Jeff
Beck</a>
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jimi+Hendrix"
class="bbcode_artist">Jimi
Hendrix</a>
were
major
influences
during
the
early
days
of
the
band,
and
later
influences
included
American
artists
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Little+Feat"
class="bbcode_artist">Little
Feat</a>
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bob+Seger"
class="bbcode_artist">Bob
Seger</a>.
