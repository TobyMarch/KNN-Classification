<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Murder_case">Murder
case</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9"
title="Edit
section:
Murder
case">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>On
April
15,
2005
three
men,
including
Cassidy,
armed
with
.45,
.40
and
9mm
caliber
pistols,
and
an
AK-47
variant
rifle,
fired
on
three
unarmed
men
during
an
argument
that

in
the
<a
href="/wiki/West_Oak_Lane,_Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="West
Oak
Lane,
Philadelphia,
Pennsylvania"
class="mw-redirect">West
Oak
Lane
neighborhood</a>
in
Northwest
Philadelphia.
Desmond
Hawkins
was
killed
by
a
shot
in
the
back.
Hawkins'
two
other
friends
were
treated
at
hospitals
for
gunshot
wounds
and
released.</p>








<p>On
June
9
a
warrant
was
issued
for
Cassidy's
arrest
on
charges
of
murder,
attempted
murder,
reckless
endangerment,
aggravated
assault,
conspiracy
and
weapons
possession.
Cassidy
surrendered
to
Philadelphia
police
on
the
afternoon
of
June
17.<sup
id="cite_ref-18"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup>
At
his
arraignment
on
Saturday,
June
18,
he
was
denied
bail,
and
sent
to
Philadelphia's
Curran-Fromhold
Correctional
Facility,
a
medium-security
prison,
where
he
would
serve
his
time
during
the
trial
and
after
he
was
sentenced.<sup
id="cite_ref-19"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Cassidy's
murder
case
took
a
turn
when
the
primary
witness
withdrew
his
confession.
This
resulted
in
the
judge
stating
that
Cassidy
would
stand
trial
for
3rd
degree
murder.
On
August
16,
2005,
Municipal
Judge
Marsha
Neifield
ruled
that
prosecutors
had
sufficient
evidence
to
charge
Cassidy
with
third-degree
murder,
attempted
murder
charges,
and
weapons
offenses.
This
was
later
overturned
to
the
original,
first
degree
charge
-
negating
the
possibility
of
parole.
On
January
25,
2006
Cassidy
was
convicted
of
involuntary
manslaughter,
two
counts
of
aggravated
assault
and
possession
of
an
instrument
of
crime
for
his
involvement
in
the
shooting.
He
was
sentenced
to
11
to
23
months
in
prison
plus
probation
and
was
credited
with
the
7
months
he
had
already
served.<sup
id="cite_ref-20"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup
id="cite_ref-21"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Cassidy
was
released
from
Pennsylvania's
Curran-Fromhold
Correctional
Facility
on
March
2,
2006
after
serving
eight
months.<sup
id="cite_ref-22"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Cassidy
talks
about
his
arrest
and
time
in
prison
in
the
2011
film
<i>Rhyme
and
Punishment</i>,
which
documents
various
Hip-Hop
artists
who
have
done
county
jail
or
state/federal
prison
time.</p>
