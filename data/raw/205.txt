<div
class="wiki-text">











of
Montreal
is
an
indie
pop
band
which
formed
in
Athens,
Georgia,
United
States
in
1997.
The
band
is
fronted
by
Kevin
Barnes,
who
writes,
composes,
and
plays
most
of
the
music
for
his
albums.










<br
/>











<br
/>
They
were
among
the
second
wave
of
groups
to
emerge
from
The
Elephant
6
Recording
Company
and
the
group
has
a
style
that
is
typical
of
many
Elephant
6
bands
due
to
their
interest
in
combining
musical
experimentation
with
the
basic
tenets
of
pop
i.e.
catchy
melodies
and
sing-along
choruses.











<span
class="hide">
The
band’s
style
has
been
influenced
by
conventional
indie
pop,
psychedelic
music,
vaudeville
and
music
hall
on
their
earlier
releases
and
by
afrobeat,
funk,
krautrock
and
reggae
on
their
recent
releases.<br
/>
<br
/>
The
band’s
style
has
been
known
to
change
between
albums.
At
first,
the
band
embraced
a
more
simple,
quirky,
lo-fi
indie
pop
sound,
which
occasionally
bordered
on
twee
pop.
The
band
was
created
by
Kevin
Barnes
and
named
after
a
failed
romance
with
a
woman
from
Montreal,
though
the
story
changes
in
various
interviews.
Barnes
was
the
only
member
of
the
“group”
prior
to
his
relocation
to
Athens,
Georgia.
There,
he
met
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Derek+Almstead"
class="bbcode_artist">Derek
Almstead</a>,
later
of
Circulatory
System,
M
Coast,
Elf
Power
etc.,
and
Bryan
Poole,
who
also
performs
as
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Late+B.P.+Helium"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Late
B.P.
Helium</a>.<br
/>
<br
/>
Together,
they
recorded
their
first
album,
<a
title="of
Montreal
-
Cherry
Peel"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/of+Montreal/Cherry+Peel"
class="bbcode_album">Cherry
Peel</a>,
as
well
as
<a
title="of
Montreal
-
The
Bird
Who
Continues
to
Eat
the
Rabbit’s
Flower"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/of+Montreal/The+Bird+Who+Continues+to+Eat+the+Rabbit%E2%80%99s+Flower"
class="bbcode_album">The
Bird
Who
Continues
to
Eat
the
Rabbit’s
Flower</a>
and
<a
title="of
Montreal
-
The
Bedside
Drama:
A
Petite
Tragedy"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/of+Montreal/The+Bedside+Drama:+A+Petite+Tragedy"
class="bbcode_album">The
Bedside
Drama:
A
Petite
Tragedy</a>.
