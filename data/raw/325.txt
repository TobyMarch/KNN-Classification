<p>Soul
singer
Dobie
Gray,
known
for
the
1973
hit
&quot;Drift
Away,&quot;
died
yesterday
at
the
age
of
71,
his
<a
href="http://www.dobiegray.com/">website
reported</a>.
The
cause
of
death
was
not
revealed.<br
/><br
/>Gray
was
born
in
1940
to
a
family
of
sharecroppers
in
Simonton,
Texas,
and
moved
out
to
California
in
the
early
Sixties.
He
met
Sonny
Bono,
then
an
A&amp;R
manager,
who
<a
href="http://www.billboard.com/news/drift-away-singer-dobie-gray-dies-1005626202.story"
target="_blank">gave
Gray
his
stage
name</a>
inspired
by
the
television
series
<em>The
Many
Loves
of
Dobie
Gillis</em>.
(His
birth
name
has
been
listed
alternately
as
Laurence
Darrow
Brown
and
Leonard
Victor
Ainsworth.)</p>











<p>Gray's
first
single,
&quot;Look
at
Me,&quot;
was
covered
by
the
Righteous
Brothers,
and
his
first
breakthrough
hit
was
&quot;The
In-Crowd&quot;
in
1965.
He
is
best
known
for
&quot;Drift
Away,&quot;
which
has
been
covered
by
Rod
Stewart,
Ray
Charles,
Waylon
Jennings
and,
more
recently,
Uncle
Kracker.
The
song
was
so
pervasive
that
it
even
inspired
a
Nancy
comic
strip
–
<a
href="http://www.dobiegray.com/biography.html"
target="_blank">featured
in
the
biography</a>
on
Gray's
website
–
that
referenced
a
frequently
misheard
part
of
the
chorus:
&quot;Gimme
the
Beach
Boys
and
free
my
soul.&quot;
(The
actual
lyric
is
&quot;Gimme
the
beat,
boys.&quot;)</p>











<p>In
recent
years,
Gray
continued
to
record
and
perform.
He
sang
the
last
verse
on
Uncle
Kracker's
2003
cover
of
&quot;Drift
Away.&quot;</p>

<h4>“Nightshift”</h4>












<span>The
Commodores&nbsp;|&nbsp;1984</span>












<p>The
year
after
soul
legends
Marvin
Gaye
and
Jackie
Wilson
died,
songwriter
Dennis
Lambert
asked
members
of
the
Commodores
to
give
him
a
tape
of
ideas.
&quot;And
the
one
from
Walter
Orange
has
this
wonderful
bass
line,&quot;
said
co-writer
Franne
Golde.
&quot;Plus
the
lyric,
'Marvin,
he
was
a
friend
of
mine'
...
Within
10
minutes,
we
had
decided
it
should
be
something
like
a
modern
R&amp;B
version
of
'Rock
'n'
Roll
Heaven,'
and
I
just
said,
'Nightshift.'&quot;
This
tribute
to
the
recently
deceased
musicians
was
the
band's
only
hit
without
Lionel
Richie,
who
had
left
for
a
solo
career.</p>
