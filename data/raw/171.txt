time
Grey,
McDowell
and
Conroy
were
also
involved
with
<a
href="/wiki/This_Mortal_Coil?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="This
Mortal
Coil">This
Mortal
Coil</a>.<sup
id="cite_ref-Strong_3-5"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-Strong-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Robbie
Grey
reformed
Modern
English
in
1989
with
Mick
Conroy
and
Aaron
Davidson
to
record
new
album,
<i>Pillow
Lips</i>,
released
in
1990
on
the
American
<a
href="/wiki/TVT_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="TVT
Records">TVT</a>
label.<sup
id="cite_ref-Strong_3-6"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-Strong-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
album
featured
a
re-recorded
&quot;I
Melt
With
You&quot;,
which
was
released
as
a
single,
and
saw
the
band
again
in
the
<i>Billboard</i>
top
100.<sup
id="cite_ref-Strong_3-7"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-Strong-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
band
split
up
for
a
second
time
in
1991,
after
contractual
problems
with
TVT,
with
Grey
forming
Engine.
In
1995,
with
the
legal
issues
with
TVT
sorted
out,
Engine
evolved
into
the
next
incarnation
of
Modern
English
and
signed
to
the
Imago
label,
with
Grey
and
Matthew
Shipley
(keyboards).
This
lineup
recorded
the
1996
album
<i>Everything's
Mad</i>.<sup
id="cite_ref-Strong_3-8"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-Strong-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Robbie
Grey
toured
the
US
with
a
new
Modern
English
lineup
from
1998–2002
and
travelled
coast
to
coast
across
the
US
and
recorded
a
new
album
with
Hugh
Jones
(producer
of
earlier
Modern
English
records).
The
songs
written
with
guitarist
Steven
Walker
(not
to
be
confused
with
the
band's
original
keyboardist)
and
including
Matthew
Shipley
came
together
on
the
road
and
back
home
in
London
between
tours.
After
a
few
years
on
the
shelf
this
collection
of
songs,
entitled
<i>Soundtrack</i>,
was
released
on
May
24,
2010
on
Darla.</p>








<p>Also
in
2010,
the
original
lineup
of
the
band
reformed
(minus
drummer
Richard
Brown)
and
toured
the
US
in
July
and
September
2010
and
the
UK
and
Paris
June
2011.
They
were
invited
by
film
director
Mark
Pellington
to
re-record
&quot;I
Melt
With
You&quot;
for
his
movie
of
the
same
name.
This
same
incarnation
of
the
band
remains
intact
and
includes
original
members
Robbie
Grey,
Mick
Conroy,
Gary
McDowell,
and
Stephen
Walker,
augmented
by
(the
&quot;other&quot;)
Steven
Walker
on
guitar
and
Ric
Chandler
on
drums.</p>
