<div
class="article_text
news">







<a
href="/artists/interpol"
class="artistLink">Interpol</a>
have
confirmed
that
they
have
started
work
on
their
fifth
studio
album.








<br
/>








<br
/>
Writing
on
their
official








<a
href="https://twitter.com/intent/user?original_referer=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.gigwise.com%2Fnews%2F83579%2Finterpol-confirm-that-theyre-working-on-new-material&amp;screen_name=Interpol&amp;tw_i=368040696969580544&amp;tw_p=tweetembed"
target="_blank_">Twitter</a>
account,
they
gave
an
update
on
their
recent
activity
by
telling
fans
they
had
been
writing
new
material.








<br
/>








<br
/>
Guitarist
Daniel
Kessler
wrote
earlier
this
week
(August
14):
&quot;We
had
a
good
rehearsal
yesterday.
Started
working
on
a
new
tune.
We
were
all
digging
it.-DK.&quot;








<br
/>








<br
/>








<img
src="http://static.nme.com/images/article/paulbanksinterpolJM031210.jpg"
align="right"
/>He
then
added
a
further
Tweet
the
following
day,
revealing:
&quot;And
wouldn't
ya
know
it...
we
started
working
on
another
new
tune
yesterday.
August
be
a
breeding
ground.-DK.&quot;








<br
/>








<br
/>
The
band
released
their
fourth
self-titled
LP
in
2010,
with
the
album
proving
to
be
their
last
record
with
former
bassist
Carlos
Dengler.
Frontman
Paul
Banks,
meanwhile,
released
his
second
solo
effort
'Banks'
last
year.
The
record,
which
was
the
follow-up
to
his
2009
debut
'Julian
Plenti
Is...
Skyscraper',
was
put
out
in
October
2012.







<br
/>








<br
/>
Interpol
re-released
their
debut
album
'Turn
On
The
Bright
Lights'
in
November
last
year
to
celebrate
its
10th
anniversary.
Discussing
the
reissue,
which
included
stacks
of
'forgotten
material',
the
band
said
in
a
statement:
&quot;We
never
could
have
imagined
that
the
album
would
have
reached
so
many
people
throughout
the
world.







<br
/>








<br
/>
&quot;We
spent
a
good
deal
of
time
this
past
year
digging
through
our
archives
and
asking
others
to
dig
through
theirs
in
the
hopes
of
uncovering
some
forgotten
material.&quot;







<br
/>








<br
/>
'Turn
On
The
Bright
Lights'
was
originally
released
in
2002,
and
was
subsequently
followed
by
'Antics'
in
2004
and
2007's
'Our
Love
To
Admire'
before
'Interpol'
followed
in
2010.








<br
/>
