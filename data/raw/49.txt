<div
class="wiki-text">











Bush
may
refer
to
at
least
2
artists:










<br
/>











<br
/>
1.
A
British
post-grunge
band










<br
/>
2.
A
Canadian
rock
band










<br
/>











<br
/>
1.
Bush
is
an
English
alternative
rock
band
formed
in
1992.
The
group
chose
the
name
“Bush”
because
they
used
to
live
in
Shepherd’s
Bush,
London.










<br
/>











<br
/>
Their
first
album,











<a
title="Bush
-
Sixteen
Stone"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/Sixteen+Stone"
class="bbcode_album">Sixteen
Stone</a>
(1994),
was
a
huge
commercial
success.
Almost
overnight,
Bush
went
from
playing
small
pubs
in
London
to
headlining
arenas
in
the
US,
the
result
of
extensive
promotion
on
MTV
and
non-stop
touring.
Critics
labeled
them
as
an
inferior
derivative
of
bands
such
as











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Nirvana"
class="bbcode_artist">Nirvana</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Pixies"
class="bbcode_artist">Pixies</a>,
and
this
criticism
followed
them
throughout
their
career
as
a
band.











<span
class="hide">
In
particular,
Rossdale’s
throaty
singing
voice
and
random,
stream
of
consciousness-style
lyrics
were
dismissed
by
some
as
an
imitation
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Nirvana"
class="bbcode_artist">Nirvana</a>’s
lead
singer,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Kurt+Cobain"
class="bbcode_artist">Kurt
Cobain</a>.
Although
the
band
reached
superstar
status
in
the
U.S.,
they
failed
to
have
much
impact
on
the
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/britpop"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">britpop</a>
riddled
UK
charts,
the
lone
exception
being
their
single
<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:0mRwCvxnUpjK15ZttHZWeI"></span><a
title="Bush
–
Swallowed"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/_/Swallowed"
class="bbcode_track">Swallowed</a>,
which
reached
#7.
A
change
in
record
labels,
management
and
an
extended
hiatus
did
not
bode
well
for
the
band,
who
unofficially
split
in
2002.<br
/>
<br
/>
On
June
21,
2010
it
was
announced
that
Bush
were
reuniting.
There
will
be
a
new
album
called
“The
Sea
of
Memories”
(previously
named
as
“Everything
Always
Now”).
Two
original
members,
Rossdale
and
Goodridge,
are
joined
by
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Chris+Traynor"
class="bbcode_artist">Chris
Traynor</a>
and
<span
title="Unknown
artist"
class="bbcode_unknown">Corey
Britz</span>.
The
release
will
be
their
first
indie
release
since
<a
title="Bush
-
Sixteen
Stone"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/Sixteen+Stone"
class="bbcode_album">Sixteen
Stone</a>,
released
on
Rossdale’s
own
Zuma
Rock
Records.
<br
/>
<br
/>
Bush’s
albums
include
<a
title="Bush
-
Razorblade
Suitcase"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/Razorblade+Suitcase"
class="bbcode_album">Razorblade
Suitcase</a>
(produced
by
Steve
Albini),
<a
title="Bush
-
Deconstructed"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/Deconstructed"
class="bbcode_album">Deconstructed</a>,
<a
title="Bush
-
The
Science
of
Things"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/The+Science+of+Things"
class="bbcode_album">The
Science
of
Things</a>,
and
<a
title="Bush
-
Golden
State"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bush/Golden+State"
class="bbcode_album">Golden
State</a>.
</span>
