<p>The

Franz
Ferdinand

10p

<a
href="/wiki/Embryo_(band)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

(band)">Embryo</a>.
Alex
Kapranos
and
Paul
Thomson
met
at
a

and
began
a
close

and
played
together
in
Yummy
Fur,
and

to
write

Kapranos
taught

Bob
Hardy,

after

by
Mick
Cooke
of
<a
href="/wiki/Belle_%26_Sebastian?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Belle
&amp;
Sebastian">Belle
&amp;
Sebastian</a>.
Kapranos
met
co-guitarist
Nick
McCarthy,
who
had
returned
to
Scotland
after
studying
<a
href="/wiki/Jazz?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Jazz">jazz</a>
bass
in
Germany,
in
2001.<sup
id="cite_ref-hot_scots_7-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hot_scots-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup>
Franz
Ferdinand's
longtime
sound
engineer
is
Michael
Parker.</p>








<p>In

the

signed
to

<a

<a

<i><a

in

<p>The
band
spent
much
of
2005
in
the
studio
in
Glasgow
working
on
their
follow-up
album,
<i>You
Could
Have
It
So
Much
Better</i>,
which
was
released
on
3
October
2005.
The
band
initially
intended
to
leave
the
album
self-titled
like
their
debut,<sup
id="cite_ref-11"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup>
but
they
changed
it
to
<i>You
Could
Have
It
So
Much
Better...With
Franz
Ferdinand</i>
before
settling
on
the
final
title.<sup
id="cite_ref-12"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
album's
cover
design
was
modelled
on
<a
href="/wiki/Alexander_Rodchenko?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Alexander
Rodchenko">Alexander
Rodchenko</a>'s
1924
portrait
of
<a
href="/wiki/Lilya_Brik?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Lilya
Brik">Lilya
Brik</a>.
The
band
attempted
to
broaden
its
musical
range
on
the
album;
Hardy
said,
&quot;There's
more
to
life
than
disco-beat
guitar
music&quot;.<sup
id="cite_ref-hot_scots_7-2"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-hot_scots-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup>
After
the
critical
acclaim
of
their
debut,
reviews
of
the
new
album
were
more
polarised,
with
some
complaints
that
the
recording
was
rushed.<sup
class="Template-Fact"
style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a
href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed"
target="_blank"
title="Wikipedia:Citation
needed"><span
title="This
claim
needs
references
to
reliable
sources.
(January
2008)">citation
needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
However,
it
was
generally
well
received
in
the
press
and
seen
as
an
album
equal
to,
or
better
than,
their
first
by
most
critics.<sup
id="cite_ref-13"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup>
It
entered
the
UK
Album
Charts
at
Number
1
and
the
US
charts
at
Number
8.
On
the
tour
that
followed
the
album
release,
Franz
Ferdinand
broke
the
record
for
the
longest
run
at
the
<a
href="/wiki/Alexandra_Palace?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Alexandra
Palace">Alexandra
Palace</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/North_London?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="North
London">North
London</a>,
by
playing
four
sold
out
nights
in
November–December
2005.<sup
class="Template-Fact"
style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a
href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed"
target="_blank"
title="Wikipedia:Citation
needed"><span
title="This
claim
needs
references
to
reliable
sources.
(January
2008)">citation
needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
To
support
the
album,
four
singles
were
released.
Included
in
that
set
is
a
<a
href="/wiki/Double_A-side?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Double
A-side"
class="mw-redirect">double
A-side</a>
single
that
contained
a
video-clip-only
single
as
well
(both
the
AA-side
&quot;L.
Wells&quot;
and
the
video-clip
&quot;Jeremy
Fraser&quot;
are
not
featured
on
the
album,
recorded
in
early
2006
during
the
band's
tour
of
Australia
in
support
of
the
album).
Also
included
is
another
video-clip-only
single
called
&quot;Wine,
In
the
Afternoon&quot;
which
is
the
B-side
to
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Eleanor_Put_Your_Boots_On?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Eleanor
Put
Your
Boots
On">Eleanor
Put
Your
Boots
On</a>&quot;,
and
was
also
not
featured
on
the
album,
but
recorded
on
tour
in
Michigan.
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Do_You_Want_To?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Do
You
Want
To">Do
You
Want
To</a>&quot;
made
it
to
number
4
and
was
declared
by
Q
to
be
the
greatest
single
of
2005,
while
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Walk_Away_(Franz_Ferdinand_song)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Walk
Away
(Franz
Ferdinand
song)">Walk
Away</a>&quot;
and
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/The_Fallen_/_L._Wells?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
Fallen
/
L.
Wells">The
Fallen&quot;
/
&quot;L.
Wells</a>&quot;
entered
the
top
15
of
the
UK
Singles
Chart.
The
fourth
and
final
single
from
the
second
album,
&quot;Eleanor
Put
Your
Boots
On&quot;,
peaked
at
number
30.</p>

<p>On
26
January
2009,
the
band
released
their
third
studio
album,
<i>Tonight:
Franz
Ferdinand</i>,
which
they
had
been
recording
in
Glasgow
since
mid-2007.<sup
id="cite_ref-14"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup>
Alex
Kapranos
has
stated
that
&quot;The
last
record
was...like
a
teenager
having
sex.
This
one's
a
bit
more
assured
and
a
bit
friendlier
for
the
dance
floor.&quot;<sup
id="cite_ref-15"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup>
Tonight
was
mixed
by
Canadian
Mix
Engineer
<a
href="/wiki/Mike_Fraser?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Mike
Fraser">Mike
Fraser</a>.<sup
id="cite_ref-16"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
song
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Ulysses_(song)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Ulysses
(song)">Ulysses</a>&quot;
was
chosen
to
be
the
first
single
and
was
released
on
19
January
2009.
It
was
first
played
by
Zane
Lowe
on
17
November
2008.
Shortly
afterwards
it
hit
YouTube.<sup
id="cite_ref-17"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup>
It
did
not
see
that
much
success
in
the
<a
href="/wiki/UK_Top_40?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="UK
Top
40"
class="mw-redirect">UK
Top
40</a>,
reaching
only
No.&nbsp;20,
but
it
fared
better
in
Spain
and
Japan
where
it
reached
No.&nbsp;2
and
No.&nbsp;3
respectively.
It
also
entered
the
Top
20
of
the
US
Modern
Rock
Chart.
The
album,
<i>Tonight</i>
was
released
26
January
2009
and
debuted
at
No.&nbsp;2
in
the
<a
href="/wiki/UK_Album_Chart?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="UK
Album
Chart"
class="mw-redirect">UK
Album
Chart</a>
and
No.&nbsp;9
in
the
<a
href="/wiki/Billboard_200?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Billboard
200">US
Billboard
200</a>.
The
second
single,
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/No_You_Girls?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="No
You
Girls">No
You
Girls</a>&quot;
saw
success
both
in
the
charts
and
on
the
radio
prior
to
release,
eventually
reaching
No.&nbsp;7
on
the
US
Modern
Rock
Chart
and
was
performed
by
Franz
Ferdinand
on
Comic
Relief
2009
Top
of
The
Pops
special.
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop_Feeling?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Can't
Stop
Feeling">Can't

was

6

&quot;What

was

a

The

&quot;What

on
<i><a
