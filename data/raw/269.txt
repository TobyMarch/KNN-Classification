<div
class="wiki-text">











Robert
Clark
“Bob”
Seger
(born
May
6,
1945)
is
an
American
rock
musician
who
achieved
his
greatest
success
in
the
1970s
and
1980s
and
continues
to
record
and
perform
today.











<br
/>











<br
/>
Seger
started
his
musical
career
in
the
1960s
in
his
native
Ann
Arbor,
Michigan,
soon
after
playing
in
and
around
Detroit
as
a
singer
and
as
the
leader
of
Bob
Seger
and
the
Last
Heard,
and
then
later
the
Bob
Seger
System.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
Best
known
for
his
work
as
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bob+Seger+&amp;+The+Silver+Bullet+Band"
class="bbcode_artist">Bob
Seger
&amp;
The
Silver
Bullet
Band</a>,
a
group
he
formed
in
1974.
Seger
was
known
as
a
workhorse
midwestern
roots-rocker
who
dealt
with
blue-collar
themes
and
toured
constantly
in
support
of
his
frequent
album
releases,
spanning
five
decades.<br
/>
<br
/>
In
April
1976,
Bob
Seger
and
the
Silver
Bullet
Band
had
an
even
bigger
commercial
breakthrough
with
the
album
Live
Bullet,
recorded
over
two
nights
in
Detroit’s
Cobo
Hall
in
September
1975.
The
album
stayed
on
the
Billboard
charts
for
168
weeks,
peaking
at
#34,
easily
Seger’s
highest
charting
album
to
that
time.
It
also
contained
Seger’s
hit
rendition
of
Tina
Turner’s
“Nutbush
City
Limits”
(#69
US)
as
well
as
Seger’s
own
classic
take
on
life
on
the
road,
“Turn
the
Page”,
from
Back
in
‘72.
It
also
harkened
back
to
his
late
1960’s
successes
with
both
“Heavy
Music”
and
“Ramblin’
Gamblin’
Man”
making
appearances.
</span>
