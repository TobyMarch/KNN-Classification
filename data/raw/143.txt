<h2

</hgroup>

<div

<div

data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











Interpol
is
a
Post-Punk
band
based
in
New
York
City,
United
States,
that
took
an
important
part
in
the











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/post-punk%20revival"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">post-punk
revival</a>
of
the
2000s.
The
band
consists
of











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Banks"
class="bbcode_artist">Paul
Banks</a>
(vocals
and
guitar),











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Sam+Fogarino"
class="bbcode_artist">Sam
Fogarino</a>
(drums),
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Daniel+Kessler"
class="bbcode_artist">Daniel
Kessler</a>
(guitar
and
backing
vocals).










<br
/>











<br
/>
The
band’s
sound
is
generally
a
mix
of
staccato
bass
and
rhythmic,
harmonized
guitar,
with
a
snare
heavy
mix,
drawing
comparisons
to
post-punk
bands
such
as











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Joy+Division"
class="bbcode_artist">Joy
Division</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Chameleons"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Chameleons</a>.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
The
band
formed
in
1998
when
Daniel
Kessler
met
drummer
<span
title="Unknown
artist"
class="bbcode_unknown">Greg
Drudy</span>,
previously
a
member
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/post-hardcore"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">post-hardcore</a>/<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/screamo"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">screamo</a>
band
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Joshua+Fit+for+Battle"
class="bbcode_artist">Joshua
Fit
for
Battle</a>
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Hot+Cross"
class="bbcode_artist">Hot
Cross</a>,
on
the
New
York
University
campus.
Kessler
later
added
Carlos
D.
on
the
bass
after
meeting
him
in
a
history
class.
The
line-up
was
complete
after
Kessler
ran
into
Paul
Banks,
a
friend
whom
he
had
met
in
Paris.
In
2000,
Sam
Fogarino,
whom
the
band
knew
through
his
job
at
a
local
record
store,
replaced
Drudy.
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Carlos+Dengler"
class="bbcode_artist">Carlos
Dengler</a>,
who
was
responsible
for
bass
and
keyboards,
left
the
band
in
2010,
and
hasn’t
been
replaced,
but
announced
various
names
to
help
them
perform
live.<br
/>
<br
/>
The
band
released
<span
class="spotify-inline-play-button
inline-play-button"
data-uri="spotify:track:0wN2CYEw0OkcJ0Dwzoosu9"></span><a
title="Interpol
–
Direction"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Interpol/_/Direction"
class="bbcode_track">Direction</a>
on
the
soundtrack
of
the
television
program
‘Six
Feet
Under’
on
June
28,
2005.
Shortly
thereafter
they
left
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/label/Matador"
class="bbcode_label">Matador</a>
for
a
major
label.<br
/>
<br
/>
<a
title="Interpol
-
Our
Love
to
Admire"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Interpol/Our+Love+to+Admire"
class="bbcode_album">Our
Love
to
Admire</a>
was
released
on
July
10,
2007
in
the
USA
(July
9
in
the
UK)
to
generally
favorable
reviews.
<a
title="Interpol
–
The
Heinrich
Maneuver"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Interpol/_/The+Heinrich+Maneuver"
class="bbcode_track">The
Heinrich
Maneuver</a>
was
its
first
single.
</span>
