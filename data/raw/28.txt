<div

<center>










<p>
<img

<p>
Twenty
years
ago
today,
Bj&ouml;rk
made
her
<em>Debut</em>,
which
landed
at
#29
on
our
list
of
the
<a
href="/features/article/best-albums-of-the-1990s">Best
Albums
of
the
1990s</a>
in
2011.
While
American
critics,
perhaps
still
beating
off
to
the
U.S.'s
exalted
alt-rock
movement,
were
divided
on
the
album
at
the
time
of
its
release,
it's
aged
remarkably
well.
And
even
if
Bj&ouml;rk
hadn't
gone
on
to
record
her
masterpieces
<a
href="/music/review/bjork-post"><em>Post</em></a>
and
<a
href="/music/review/bjork-homogenic"><em>Homogenic</em></a>,
<em>Debut</em>
was
enough
to
cement
her
legacy
as
one
of
pop's
most
forward-thinking
performers.
And
that
includes
her
contributions
to
the
music
video
form.</p>









<p>
<strong>10.
&quot;Human
Behaviour&quot;
(Dir:
Michel
Gondry)</strong>.
Bj&ouml;rk's
very
first
music
video
as
a
solo
artist
was
also
the
start
of
a
fruitful
professional
relationship
with
innovative
director
Michel
Gondry,
who
would
go
on
to
helm
a
total
of
eight
videos
for
the
singer.
&quot;Human
Behaviour&quot;
isn't
their
most
successful
collaboration,
but
it
literally
set
the
stage
for
both
their
careers.</p>









<center>










<object><iframe
allowfullscreen=""
frameborder="0"
height="315"
src="//www.youtube.com/embed/GCGveA39VYA"
width="560"></iframe></object>








</center>









<p>
<strong>9.
&quot;Mutual
Core&quot;
(Dir:
Andrew
Thomas
Huang)</strong>.
Eric
Henderson
calls
this
video
&quot;little
tectonic
plate
of
horrors.&quot;
The
lyrics
to
&quot;Mutual
Core&quot;
sometimes
feel
like
Bj&ouml;rk
is
reading
from
a
science
textbook
(&quot;As
fast
as
your
fingernail
grows/The
Atlantic
Ridge
drifts&quot;),
but
the
video,
a
sort
of
sequel
to
the
Gondry-directed
1997
clip
for
<a
href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2BSMcVRgloY"
target="_blank">&quot;J&oacute;ga,&quot;</a>
brings
the
song
to
explosive
life,
with
Bj&ouml;rk,
naturally,
in
the
role
of
neglected
Mother
Nature.</p>









<center>










<object><iframe
allowfullscreen=""
frameborder="0"
height="315"
src="//www.youtube.com/embed/ZM80F_J-QHE"
width="560"></iframe></object>








</center>









<p>
<strong>8.
&quot;Isobel&quot;
(Dir:
Gondry)</strong>.
While
making
suggestions
for
this
list,
Ed
Gonzalez
referred
to
the
surreal,
visually
striking
&quot;Isobel,&quot;
Bj&ouml;rk's
third
video
with
Gondry,
as
&quot;Jean
Epstein-ian
kabuki
horror.&quot;</p>









<center>










<object><iframe
allowfullscreen=""
frameborder="0"
height="420"
src="//www.youtube.com/embed/S1QtZqCiP7s"
width="560"></iframe></object>








</center>









<p>
<strong>7.
&quot;Triumph
of
a
Heart&quot;
(Dir:
Spike
Jonze)</strong>.
To
quote
Gonzalez
again,
this
time
from
our
list
of
the
<a
href="/features/article/best-of-the-aughts-music-videos">Best
Music
Videos
of
the
Aughts</a>:
&quot;Letting
off
steam
has
never
felt
so
touchingly
conveyed
as
it
does
in
this
quirky
and
unexpectedly
poetic
rumination
on
the
nature
of
affection
and
dependency.&quot;</p>









<center>










<object><iframe
allowfullscreen=""
frameborder="0"
height="420"
src="//www.youtube.com/embed/LoP-YTjN7PA"
width="560"></iframe></object>








</center>









<p>
<strong>6.
&quot;Pagan
Poetry&quot;
(Dir:
Nick
Knight)</strong>.
Long
before
Lady
Gaga
donned
Alexander
McQueen
and
hired
Nick
Knight
to
direct
her
&quot;Born
This
Way&quot;
music
video,
Bj&ouml;rk
was
stirring
up
controversy
with
&quot;Pagan
Poetry,&quot;
which
was
banned
by
MTV
in
2001.
About
a
woman
preparing
herself
for
marriage,
the
clip
features
scenes
of
oral
sex
reportedly
shot
privately
by
the
singer
herself
and
a
topless
Bj&ouml;rk
literally
sewing
herself
into
her
gown.</p>









<center>










<object><iframe
allowfullscreen=""
frameborder="0"
height="420"
src="//www.youtube.com/embed/KqF8_UcUQdQ"
width="560"></iframe></object>








</center>
