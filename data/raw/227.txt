<div

<div

<div
class="wiki-text">











Pixies
are
an
American











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/alternative%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">alternative
rock</a>
band
which
formed
in
Boston,
Massachusetts,
United
States
in
1986.
The
band
consists
of
Charles
(










<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Black+Francis"
class="bbcode_artist">Black
Francis</a>)
Thompson
IV
(vocals,
guitar;
also
known
as
‘’










<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Frank+Black"
class="bbcode_artist">Frank
Black</a>’‘),











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Joey+Santiago"
class="bbcode_artist">Joey
Santiago</a>
(lead
guitar)
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/David+Lovering"
class="bbcode_artist">David
Lovering</a>
(drums).
Until
2013,
the
band
also
featured











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Kim+Deal"
class="bbcode_artist">Kim
Deal</a>
on
bass
and
vocals.
The
band
officially
split
in
1993
and
reunited
in
2004.
They
went
on
hiatus
in
2007,
with
each
band
member
pursuing
projects
of
their
own,
only
to
reunite
in
2009,
with
the
announcement
of
a
new
tour
commencing
in
Europe
that
summer.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
Although
quite
popular
on
alternative
rock
radio
in
the
United
States
due
to
heavy
airplay
of
the
singles
“Monkey
Gone
to
Heaven”
and
“Here
Comes
Your
Man”,
they
were
never
widely
popular
there,
but
found
success
in
Europe,
especially
in
the
UK
(where
they
managed
three
top
40
singles
“Velouria”,
“Planet
of
Sound”
and
a
re-release
of
“Debaser”)
and
Belgium
and
the
Netherlands,
where
they
were
heavily
broadcasted
by
alternative
public
radio.
<br
/>
<br
/>
The
band’s
style
of
alternative
rock
music
is
heavily
influenced
by
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/punk"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">punk</a>
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/surf%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">surf
rock</a>,
and
while
highly
melodic,
is
capable
of
being
tremendously
abrasive
at
the
same
time.
Francis
is
the
Pixies’
primary
songwriter
and
singer
and
has
a
distinctly
desperate,
yowling
delivery.
He
has
typically
written
cryptic
songs
about
offbeat
subjects,
such
as
UFOs
and
surrealism.
References
to
mental
instability,
violent
Biblical
imagery,
and
physical
injury
feature
in
many
of
the
band’s
songs.
</span>
