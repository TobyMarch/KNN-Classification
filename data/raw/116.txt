play">EP</a>
entitled
<i><a
href="/wiki/On_Golden_Smog?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="On
Golden
Smog">On
Golden
Smog</a></i>.
All
of
the
band
members
were
credited
under
pseudonyms
for
this
release
as
a
result
of
contractual
obligations
to
other
record
companies.
Guest
vocalist
Bill
Sullivan
just
didn't
want
his
name
on
the
project.</p>








<p>Over
the
next
few
years,
members
of
Golden
Smog
began
adding
original
material
to
the
group's
repertoire,
although
cover
songs
would
continue
to
be
featured
in
concert
and
on
record
throughout
their
career.</p>








<p>In
1995,
the
group
released
its
full-length
debut,
<i><a
href="/wiki/Down_by_the_Old_Mainstream?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Down
by
the
Old
Mainstream">Down
by
the
Old
Mainstream</a></i>
(recorded
at
<a
href="/wiki/Pachyderm_Studio?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Pachyderm
Studio">Pachyderm
Recording
Studio</a>),
consisting
of
mostly
original
songs,
with
a
handful
of
covers.
By
this
time,
Mars
had
left
Golden
Smog
and
the
band
consisted
of
Johnson,
Murphy,
Louris,
and
Perlman,
along
with
two
new
members:
Wilco
frontman
<a
href="/wiki/Jeff_Tweedy?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Jeff
Tweedy">Jeff
Tweedy</a>
and
Honeydogs
drummer
<a
href="/wiki/Noah_Levy?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Noah
Levy">Noah
Levy</a>.
As
with
the
band's
debut
EP,
all
of
the
band
members
were
credited
under
pseudonyms
(which
consisted
of
their
middle
names
and
the
names
of
the
streets
where
they
grew
up)
as
performers—although
they
all
used
their
real
names
in
the
writing
credits.</p>








<p>At
a
New
Year's
Eve
show
in
1996/97
<a
href="/wiki/Jody_Stephens?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Jody
Stephens">Jody
Stephens</a>
(of
<a
href="/wiki/Big_Star_(band)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Big
Star
(band)"
class="mw-redirect">Big
Star</a>)
took
over
for
Noah
on
the
drums,
and
subsequently
became
a
full
band
member.
Golden
Smog
then
released
their
second
full-length
studio
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/Weird_Tales_(album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Weird
Tales
(album)">Weird
Tales</a></i>
in
1998,
with
all
band
members
credited
under
their
real
names
as
both
writers
and
performers.</p>








<h3><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Current_incarnation_.282005.E2.80.93present.29">Current
incarnation
(2005–present)</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Golden_Smog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4"
title="Edit
section:
Current
incarnation
(2005–present)">edit</a>]</span></h3>








<p>After
a
period
of
inactivity,
in
2005
a
new
incarnation
of
Golden
Smog
formed
and
recorded
the
<i><a
href="/wiki/Another_Fine_Day?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Another
Fine
Day">Another
Fine
Day</a></i>
album
in
the
village
of
<a
href="/wiki/El_Puerto_de_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="El
Puerto
de
Santa
Mar&iacute;a">El
Puerto
de
Santa
Mar&iacute;a</a>,
Spain,
produced
by
<a
href="/wiki/Paco_Loco?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Paco
Loco">Paco
Loco</a>.
At
this
point
the
band
consisted
of
Johnson,
Louris,
Murphy
and
Perlman;
guest
performers
on
several
tracks
included
Tweedy,
Spanish
female
vocalist
<a
href="/wiki/Muni_Cam%C3%B3n?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Muni
Cam&oacute;n">Muni
Cam&oacute;n</a>,
drummers
<a
href="/wiki/Linda_Pitmon?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Linda
Pitmon"
class="mw-redirect">Linda
Pitmon</a>
and
Jody
Stephens,
and
multi-instrumentalist
Ed
Ackerson.</p>








<p>In
the
summer
of
2006,
the
Johnson/Louris/Murphy/Perlman
version
of
Golden
Smog
undertook
a
concert
tour,
augmented
by
a
touring
keyboardist
and
drummer.
This
same
line-up
of
the
band
then
issued
<i><a
href="/wiki/Blood_on_the_Slacks?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Blood
on
the
Slacks">Blood
on
the
Slacks</a></i>
in
2007;
with
no
permanent
drummer,
various
drummers
filled
the
position
on
this
disc,
including
Pitmon,
Ackerson,
Peter
Anderson
and
even
Marc
Perlman.</p>
