<h4>
Biography
</h4>








<div
more-text="More"
will-truncate-max-height="72">








<p
class="extra">Southern
rap
group
Dem
Franchize
Boyz
includes
members
Gerald
&quot;Buddie&quot;
Tiller,
Bernard
&quot;Jizzal
Man&quot;
Leverette,
Maurice
&quot;Parlae&quot;
Gleaton,
and
Jamall
&quot;Pimpin&quot;
Willingham.
After
a
period
spent
pushing
low-key
releases
and
mixtapes,
the
Atlanta-based
group
was
picked
up
by
Universal
and
released
a
self-titled
album
in
September
of
2004,
preceded
by
&quot;White
Tee&quot;
—
a
regional
smash
that
received
nationwide
exposure
on
BET.
When
Jermaine
Dupri
and
his
So
So
Def
label
moved
from
Arista
to
Virgin
(Dupri
had
been
appointed
president
of
Virgin's
urban
division),
one
of
his
first
acquisitions
was
DFB,
who
soon
scored
a
number
one
R&amp;B
hit
with
a
remix
of
the
first
album's
&quot;Oh
I
Think
Dey
Like
Me&quot;
(retitled
&quot;I
Think
They
Like
Me,&quot;
with
guest
appearances
from
Dupri,
Bow
Wow,
and
Da
Brat).
This
set
the
stage
for
the
Top
Ten
album
On
Top
of
Our
Game,
released
in
early
2006.
Follow-up
single
&quot;Lean
Wit
It,
Rock
Wit
It&quot;
made
it
known
that
they
were
leaders
in
the
&quot;snap
music&quot;
movement
(Ying
Yang
Twins'
&quot;Wait,&quot;
David
Banner's
&quot;Play,&quot;
and
D4L's
&quot;Laffy
Taffy&quot;).
A
screwed-and-chopped
version
of
the
album
was
released
soon
after.
</p>
