George
Harrison,
MBE
(25
February
1943
–
29
November
2001)
born
in
Liverpool,
UK,
was
an
English
rock
musician,
singer-songwriter
and
film
producer
who
achieved
international
fame
as
lead
guitarist
in











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Beatles</a>,
along
with
mainstream
success
as
a
solo
artist.
George
contributed
to
the
songwriting
talents
of
his
Beatles
band
mates,











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/John+Lennon"
class="bbcode_artist">John
Lennon</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+McCartney"
class="bbcode_artist">Paul
McCartney</a>,
firstly
by
adding
ideas
and
guitar
parts,
and
later
with
his
distinctive
style
of
music
inspired
by
Eastern
spirituality.
Ten
years
after
Harrison’s
death,
and
forty-two
years
after
its
first
release,
his
classic
“










<a
title="The
Beatles
–
Here
Comes
the
Sun"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles/_/Here+Comes+the+Sun"
class="bbcode_track">Here
Comes
the
Sun</a>”
is
the
number
one
Beatles
download
on
iTunes.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
After
the
Beatles
stopped
touring
in
1966,
George
started
writing
more
songs
than
could
be
fitted
onto
the
albums
so
that
by
the
time
of
the
split
he
had
built
up
a
collection
of
songs,
some
used
on
his
debut
solo
album,
<a
title="George
Harrison
-
All
Things
Must
Pass"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/George+Harrison/All+Things+Must+Pass"
class="bbcode_album">All
Things
Must
Pass</a>.
The
album,
released
in
1970,
contains
the
two-time
number
one
hit,
“<a
title="George
Harrison
–
My
Sweet
Lord"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/George+Harrison/_/My+Sweet+Lord"
class="bbcode_track">My
Sweet
Lord</a>”.<br
/>
<br
/>
Harrison
is
also
credited
for
creating
music
benefits,
notably
the
1971
<a
title="George
Harrison
-
Concert
for
Bangladesh"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/George+Harrison/Concert+for+Bangladesh"
class="bbcode_album">Concert
for
Bangladesh</a>.
Harrison
played
beside
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bob+Dylan"
class="bbcode_artist">Bob
Dylan</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Eric+Clapton"
class="bbcode_artist">Eric
Clapton</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Billy+Preston"
class="bbcode_artist">Billy
Preston</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Ringo+Starr"
class="bbcode_artist">Ringo
Starr</a>,
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Leon+Russell"
class="bbcode_artist">Leon
Russell</a>
to
a
crowd
of
40,000
in
Madison
Square
Garden,
New
York.
The
show
raised
over
$240,000
benefiting
the
George
Harrison
Fund
for
Bangladesh
relief.<br
/>
<br
/>
Harrison
went
on
to
release
several
successful
solo
albums,
and
in
the
late
1980s
collaborated
with
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bob+Dylan"
class="bbcode_artist">Bob
Dylan</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jeff+Lynne"
class="bbcode_artist">Jeff
Lynne</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Roy+Orbison"
class="bbcode_artist">Roy
Orbison</a>,
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Tom+Petty"
class="bbcode_artist">Tom
Petty</a>,
in
a
group
called
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Traveling+Wilburys"
class="bbcode_artist">Traveling
Wilburys</a>.
The
group
released
two
albums.
Later,
Harrison
reunited
with
the
other
two
surviving
Beatles,
after
John
Lennon’s
murder
in
1980,
to
produce
two
final
Beatles
records,
“<a
title="The
Beatles
–
Free
as
a
Bird"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles/_/Free+as+a+Bird"
class="bbcode_track">Free
as
a
Bird</a>”
and
“<a
title="The
Beatles
–
Real
Love"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles/_/Real+Love"
class="bbcode_track">Real
Love</a>”
featuring
the
voice
of
Lennon,
taken
from
home
demos
provided
by
Lennon’s
widow,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Yoko+Ono"
class="bbcode_artist">Yoko
Ono</a>.
</span>
