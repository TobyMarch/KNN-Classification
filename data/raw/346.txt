onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Light
music">light
orchestra</a>&quot;
(orchestras
with
only
a
few
cellos
and
violins
that
were
popular
in
Britain
during
the
1960s).
The
official
band
logo,
designed
in
1976
by
artist
<a
href="/wiki/John_Kosh?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="John
Kosh">Kosh</a>,
was
first
seen
on
their
1976
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/A_New_World_Record?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="A
New
World
Record">A
New
World
Record</a></i>
and
is
based
on
a
1946
<a
href="/wiki/Wurlitzer?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Wurlitzer">Wurlitzer</a>
<a
href="/wiki/Jukebox?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Jukebox">jukebox</a>
model
4008
speaker.
The
4008
speaker
was
itself
based
upon
the
upper
cabinet
of
the
<a
href="/wiki/Wurlitzer?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Wurlitzer">Wurlitzer</a>
model
1015
<a
href="/wiki/Jukebox?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Jukebox">jukebox</a>.<sup
id="cite_ref-24"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup>
The
band's
previous
logo,
designed
by
John
Kehe
at
United
Artists
Records,
took
its
cues
from
the
<a
href="/wiki/General_Electric?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="General
Electric">General
Electric</a>
logo.
The
new
logo
appeared
on
most
of
the
band's
subsequent
album
covers
in
various
forms.
For
instance,
on
1977's
<i><a
href="/wiki/Out_of_the_Blue_(Electric_Light_Orchestra_album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Out
of
the
Blue
(Electric
Light
Orchestra
album)">Out
of
the
Blue</a></i>,
the
logo
was
turned
into
a
huge
flying
saucer
space
station,
an
enduring
image
now
synonymous
with
the
band.
On
the
follow-up
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/Discovery_(Electric_Light_Orchestra_album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Discovery
(Electric
Light
Orchestra
album)">Discovery</a></i>,
the
logo
became
a
small
glowing
artifact
on
top
of
a
treasure
chest.
Bev
Bevan
usually
displayed
the
logo
on
his
drum
kit.<br
style="clear:both;"
/></p>
