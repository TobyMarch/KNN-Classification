<h2
class="rm">Biography</h2>









</hgroup>








</header>








<div
class="wiki">









<div
id="wikiAbstract"
class="wiki-abstract"
data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











During
the
transitional
period
of
the
early
1970s,











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Kantner"
class="bbcode_artist">Paul
Kantner</a>
(founding
member
of











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jefferson+Airplane"
class="bbcode_artist">Jefferson
Airplane</a>)
recorded
Blows
Against
the
Empire,
a
concept
album
featuring
an
ad
hoc
group
of
musicians
that
he
dubbed
Jefferson
Starship,
marking
the
first
use
of
that
name.
This
edition
of
Jefferson
Starship
(such
as
it
was)
included
members
of











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Crosby,+Stills,+Nash+&amp;+Young"
class="bbcode_artist">Crosby,
Stills,
Nash
&amp;
Young</a>
(










<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/David+Crosby"
class="bbcode_artist">David
Crosby</a>
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Graham+Nash"
class="bbcode_artist">Graham
Nash</a>)
and
members
of











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Grateful+Dead"
class="bbcode_artist">Grateful
Dead</a>
(










<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jerry+Garcia"
class="bbcode_artist">Jerry
Garcia</a>,











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Phil+Lesh"
class="bbcode_artist">Phil
Lesh</a>,











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bill+Kreutzmann"
class="bbcode_artist">Bill
Kreutzmann</a>,
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Mickey+Hart"
class="bbcode_artist">Mickey
Hart</a>),
as
well
as
some
of
the
remaining
members
of











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jefferson+Airplane"
class="bbcode_artist">Jefferson
Airplane</a>
(










<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Grace+Slick"
class="bbcode_artist">Grace
Slick</a>,











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Marty+Balin"
class="bbcode_artist">Marty
Balin</a>,











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Joey+Covington"
class="bbcode_artist">Joey
Covington</a>,
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jack+Casady"
class="bbcode_artist">Jack
Casady</a>).











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
By
1973,
after
the
breakup
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jefferson+Airplane"
class="bbcode_artist">Jefferson
Airplane</a>,
with
Kaukonen
and
Casady
now
devoting
their
full
attention
to
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Hot+Tuna"
class="bbcode_artist">Hot
Tuna</a>,
several
of
the
musicians
who
worked
on
Kantner,
Slick,
and
Freiberg’s
album
“Baron
von
Tollbooth
&amp;
the
Chrome
Nun”
formed
the
core
of
a
new
lineup
that
was
formally
reborn
as
“Jefferson
Starship”
in
1974.
Kantner,
Slick,
and
Freiberg
were
charter
members.
The
line-up
also
included
late-Airplane
holdovers
drummer
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/John+Barbata"
class="bbcode_artist">John
Barbata</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Marty+Balin"
class="bbcode_artist">Marty
Balin</a>,and
fiddler
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Papa+John+Creach"
class="bbcode_artist">Papa
John
Creach</a>
(who
also
played
with
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Hot+Tuna"
class="bbcode_artist">Hot
Tuna</a>),
along
with
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Sears"
class="bbcode_artist">Pete
Sears</a>
(who,
like
Freiberg,
played
bass
and
keyboards)
and
twenty-year-old
guitarist
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Craig+Chaquico"
class="bbcode_artist">Craig
Chaquico</a>.<br
/>
Eventually,
Slick’s
alcoholism
became
a
problem,
which
led
to
two
nights
of
disastrous
concerts
in
Germany
in
1978.
The
first
night,
fans
ransacked
the
stage
when
Slick
failed
to
appear.
The
following
night,
Slick,
in
a
drunken
stupor,
shocked
the
audience
by
using
profanity
and
sexual
references
throughout
most
of
her
songs.
</span>
