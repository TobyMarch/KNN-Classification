<h3

<div

<div

data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











Creedence
Clearwater
Revival
(sometimes
shortened
to
Creedence
or
CCR)
was
an
American
rock
band
that
gained
popularity
in
the
late
1960s
and
early
1970s
with
a
number
of
successful
singles
drawn
from
various
albums.
The
band
consisted
of
lead
vocalist,
lead
guitarist,
and
primary
songwriter











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/John+Fogerty"
class="bbcode_artist">John
Fogerty</a>,
his
brother
and
rhythm
guitarist
Tom
Fogerty,
bassist
Stu
Cook,
and
drummer
Doug
Clifford.











<span
class="hide">
Their
musical
style
encompassed
country
rock
and
swamp
rock
genres;
it
remains
a
staple
of
radio
airplay
worldwide.
They
were
inducted
into
the
Rock
and
Roll
Hall
of
Fame
in
1993
and
were
ranked
at
82
on
Rolling
Stone’s
list
of
the
100
greatest
artists
of
all
time.<br
/>
<br
/>
The
band
started
out
as
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Blue+Velvets"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Blue
Velvets</a>,
formed
by
John
Fogerty,
Doug
Clifford,
and
Stu
Cook
in
El
Cerrito,
California,
United
States
in
the
late
1950s.
They
were
an
instrumental
trio,
however
during
the
early
’60s
they
began
backing
Tom
Fogerty,
John’s
older
brother,
for
school
dances
at
El
Cerrito
High
School,
on
fraternity
house
gigs
and
in
the
recording
studio.
By
the
mid
1960s,
the
band
signed
a
contract
with
Fantasy
Records
which
was
a
local
label
based
in
San
Francisco
at
that
time.
They
were
attracted
to
the
label
because
Fantasy
had
released
a
national
hit
by
Vince
Guaraldi,
“Cast
Your
Fate
To
The
Wind”.
</span>
