<h4>
Biography
</h4>








<div
more-text="More"
will-truncate-max-height="72">








<p
class="extra">The
summery
hooks
and
warm
lyrics
of
Modern
English's
biggest
hit,
&quot;I
Melt
With
You,&quot;
gave
listeners
the
impression
that
the
band
was
an
upbeat
pop
act
in
the
early
'80s.
&quot;I
Melt
With
You&quot;
was
actually
an
anomaly
in
Modern
English's
early
discography.
Formed
in
Colchester,
England,
in
1979,
Modern
English
was
originally
a
punk
group
called
the
Lepers.
Featuring
Robbie
Grey
(vocals,
guitar),
Gary
McDowell
(guitar),
and
Richard
Brown
(drums),
the
Lepers
mainly
performed
at
parties.
After
Mick
Conroy
(bass)
and
Stephen
Walker
(keyboards)
joined
the
band,
they
changed
their
name
to
Modern
English
and
were
signed
to
4AD
Records.
Inspired
by
the
stylish
gloom
of
Bauhaus
and
Joy
Division,
Modern
English
released
the
singles
&quot;Swans
on
Glass&quot;
and
&quot;Gathering
Dust&quot;
before
recording
their
1981
debut
LP
Mesh
&amp;
Lace.
Boiling
with
raw
anger,
dissonant
rhythms,
and
weird
noises,
Mesh
&amp;
Lace
confused
some
U.K.
critics
while
mesmerizing
others.
A
year
later,
the
group
streamlined
their
sound,
dropping
much
of
Mesh
&amp;
Lace's
gothic
experimentation
on
After
the
Snow.
&quot;I
Melt
With
You&quot;
was
included
on
the
Valley
Girl
soundtrack,
and
its
video
became
an
MTV
staple.
Although
&quot;I
Melt
With
You&quot;
didn't
reach
the
Top
40
charts
in
America,
After
the
Snow
sold
more
than
500,000
copies.
However,
the
band's
next
album,
1984's
Ricochet
Days,
was
a
flop.
Pressured
by
their
U.S.
label
Sire
Records
to
release
another
hit
and
exhausted
from
touring,
Modern
English
began
falling
apart;
Walker
and
Brown
were
fired
from
the
group.
Grey
continued
recording
with
different
Modern
English
lineups
releasing
the
albums
Pillow
Lips
in
1990
and
Everything
Is
Mad
in
1996.
Also
in
the
early
'90s,
&quot;I
Melt
With
You&quot;
was
played
in
a
successful
Burger
King
ad.
In
2010,
Modern
English
returned
with
the
full-length
studio
effort
Soundtrack
featuring
production
from
After
the
Snow
producer
Hugh
Jones.
</p>
