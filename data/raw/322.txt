<p>Soul-pop
singer
<a

a
top
5

in
1973
with
the

&quot;Drift
Away,&quot;
has
died.
The
news
came
on
the
evening
of
Dec.
6
via
his
official
website,
although
no
cause
of
death
was
listed.
He
was
71</p>

















<p>Born
near
Houston
in
1940
to
a
family
of
sharecroppers,
Gray,
whose
real
name
has
been
listed
as
Leonard
Victor
Ainsworth
and
Laurence
Darrow
Brown,
discovered
gospel
music
through
his
grandfather,
a
Baptist
minister.
He
moved
to
Los
Angeles
in
the
early
1960s
where
he
met
Sonny
Bono,
who
dubbed
him
Dobie
Gray
after
the
then
popular
sitcom
The
Many
Loves
of
Dobie
Gillis.</p>

















<p>Gray
saw
his
first
music
industry
success
in
1965
with
the
single
&quot;The
'In'
Crowd,&quot;
which
reached
No.
13
on
the
charts,
but
it
was
1973's
country-soul
&quot;Drift
Away,&quot;
whose
chorus
pined,
&quot;I
wanna
get
lost
in
your
rock
and
roll
and
drift
away,&quot;
that
would
make
him
a
radio
staple
for
decades
to
come.</p>

<p>The
