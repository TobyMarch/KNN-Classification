<h3><span

<p>Tom
Scholz
first
started
writing

in

<a

of

&quot;Foreplay&quot;.<sup
id="cite_ref-bioofficial_4-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-bioofficial-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
While

the

&quot;Freehold&quot;,

<a

<a

After

class="reference"><a

<a

onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

in

<a

Brad
Delp
on

Barry

on

on

.

class="reference"><a

to

&quot;<a
href="/wiki/More_Than_a_Feeling?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

Than

Feeling">More
Than
a
Feeling</a>&quot;,
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Peace_of_Mind_(Boston_song)?qsrc=3044"

of

(Boston

&quot;<a

&quot;Something

hits
such
as
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/More_Than_a_Feeling?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="More
Than
a
Feeling">More
Than
a
Feeling</a>&quot;.<sup
id="cite_ref-43"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>








<p>Guitarist
and
primary
song
writer
Tom
Scholz'
blend
of
musical
styles,
ranging
from
classical
to
1960s
English
pop,
has
resulted
in
a
unique
sound,
most
consistently
realized
on
the
first
two
albums
(<i>Boston</i>
and
<i>Don't
Look
Back</i>).
This
sound
is
characterized
by
multiple
lead
and
blended
<a
href="/wiki/Harmony?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Harmony">harmonies</a>
guitar
work
(usually
harmonized
in
thirds),
often
alternating
between
and
then
mixing
<a
href="/wiki/Electric_guitar?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Electric
guitar">electric</a>
and
<a
href="/wiki/Steel-string_acoustic_guitar?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Steel-string
acoustic
guitar">acoustic
guitars</a>.
The
band's
harmonic
style
has
been
characterized
as
being
&quot;violin-like&quot;
without
using
synthesizers.<sup
id="cite_ref-44"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup>
Scholz
is
well-regarded
for
the
development
of
complex,
multi-tracked
guitar
harmonies.
Another
contributing
factor
is
the
use
of
handmade,
<a
href="/wiki/High_tech?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="High
tech">high
tech</a>
equipment,
such
as
the
<a
href="/wiki/Rockman_(music)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Rockman
(music)"
class="mw-redirect">Rockman</a>,
used
by
artists
such
as
<a
href="/wiki/Journey_(band)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Journey
(band)">Journey</a>
guitarist
<a
href="/wiki/Neal_Schon?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Neal
Schon">Neal
Schon</a>,
the
band
<a
href="/wiki/ZZ_Top?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="ZZ
Top">ZZ
Top</a>,
and
<a
href="/wiki/Ted_Nugent?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Ted
Nugent">Ted
Nugent</a>.
<a
href="/wiki/Def_Leppard?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Def
Leppard">Def
Leppard</a>'s
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/Hysteria_(Def_Leppard_album)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Hysteria
(Def
Leppard
album)">Hysteria</a></i>
was
created
using
only
Rockman
<a
href="/wiki/Technology?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Technology">technology</a>.
Scholz'
production
style
combines
deep,
aggressive,
comparatively
short
guitar
<a
href="/wiki/Riff?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Riff"
class="mw-redirect">riffing</a>
and
nearly
<a
href="/wiki/Ethereal_Wave?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Ethereal
Wave"
class="mw-redirect">ethereal</a>,
generally
longer
note
vocal
harmonies.
A
heavier,
lower
and
darker
overall
approach
came
in
the
next
two
albums
(<i>Third
Stage</i>
and
<i>Walk
On</i>).
The
original
track,
&quot;Higher
Power&quot;,
on
the
<i>Greatest
Hits</i>
album
exhibits
a
near
<a
href="/wiki/Neue_Deutsche_H%C3%A4rte?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Neue
Deutsche
H&auml;rte">Germanic</a>,
almost
techno
influence
with
its
sequencer-sounding
keyboards,
a
sound
most
fully
realized
on
<i>Corporate
America'</i>s
title
track.</p>








<p>Singer
Brad
Delp,
who
was
strongly
influenced
by
the
<a
href="/wiki/The_Beatles?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="The
Beatles">Beatles</a>,<sup
id="cite_ref-45"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup>
is
also
credited
for
helping
to
create
Boston's
sound
with
his
signature
vocal
style.</p>
