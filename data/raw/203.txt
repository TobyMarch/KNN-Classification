<p>The
band's
style
has
been
known
to
change
between

At

a
more

<a

<a

pop</a>.

to

fuller
sound,

concept
albums
<i>The
Gay
Parade</i>
and
its
follow-up,
<i>Coquelicot
Asleep
in
the
Poppies:
A
Variety
of
Whimsical
Verse</i>.

albums
contain
more
narrative
lyrics,
and
often
imitating
the
style
of
old
1950s
<a
href="/wiki/Radio_plays?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Radio
plays"
class="mw-redirect">radio
plays</a>.</p>








<p>2004's
<i>Satanic
Panic
in
the
Attic</i>
marked
the

of
an
evolving
change
in
style.

shifted
to

still
with

guitars
on
most
tracks,
with
more
traditional
pop
and
rock
structures,
to
be
further
advanced
in
later
albums
and
new
songs.
In
their
most
recent
releases
and
concerts,
the
band
has
fully
embraced
a
sort
of
techno-pop
glam
image,
with
little
of
their
previous
incarnations
surfacing.</p>
