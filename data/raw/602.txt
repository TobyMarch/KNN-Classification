<h2
class="hed6">Ishmael
Butler
on
Digable
Planets:
'I
think
it's
the
end'</h2>







<p
class="byline">Posted
by
<a
href="http://search.nwsource.com/search?searchtype=cq&amp;sort=date&amp;from=ST&amp;source=ST&amp;byline=Andrew
Matson">Andrew
Matson</a></p>







<div
class="clear"></div>







<!--
<div
style="clear:both;"></div>
-->







<div
class="body">








<p><a
href="http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/matsononmusic/11162.jpeg"><img
alt="11162.jpeg"
src="http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/matsononmusic/assets_c/2012/12/11162-thumb-630x417-36856.jpeg"
width="630"
height="417"
style=""
/></a><br
/>
<em>Image
by
Leif
Podhajsky
/
original
photo
by
David
Belisle</em></p>








<p>There
are
hardly
any
active
rappers/producers
who've
been
successful
in
different
eras,
but
Seattle's
Ishmael
Butler
is
one
of
them.
His
old
trio
Digable
Planets
<a
href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blXFQcr5snI"><strong>won
a
Grammy</strong></a>
back
in
1994
for
their
jazzy
song
&quot;Rebirth
of
Slick
(Cool
Like
Dat),&quot;
back
when
Butler
was
called
Butterfly,
joined
by
rappers
Doodlebug
and
Ladybug
Mecca.
Now
he's
Palaceer
Lazaro
or
just
Ish
in
his
current
duo
Shabazz
Palaces,
which
stands
on
the
artistic
cutting-edge
with
a
sci-fi/African
style.
Seattle
almost
got
back-to-back
concerts
from
both
acts
at
<a
href="http://stgpresents.org"><strong>the
Neptune
Theatre</strong></a>
Dec.
28
and
29
&#8212;
which
would
have
been
rare
and
very
cool.
But
at
the
last
minute,
Digable
Planets
canceled.
</p>








<p>Butler
called
me
to
explain
why
(see
partial,
edited
transcript
below).
He
said
it
was
Ladybug
Mecca's
fault
for
being
difficult
to
work
with,
and
he
does
not
see
a
future
for
Digable
Planets,
calling
the
situation
&quot;a
heartbreaker.&quot;
<br
/>
</p>








<a
name="continue"></a>







<p><br
/>
Digable
Planets
has
been
through
this
before.
They
toured
with
a
Mecca
stand-in
for
a
while,
Lady
Madarocka,
but
Butler
didn't
want
to
do
that
again:
&quot;I
didn't
want
to
compromise
the
integrity
of
the
originality
of
the
group,
truth
be
told.&quot;
Mecca
did
not
respond
to
my
email
asking
for
her
side
of
the
story.
</p>








<p>As
it
stands,
the
Neptune
hosts
Shabazz
Palaces
with
THEESatisfaction
Dec.
28
(look
out
for
my
concert
preview
soon).
The
following
night,
Digable
Planets
has
been
replaced
with
hardcore
Detroit
rapper
Danny
Brown.
Previously
bought
tickets
will
be
honored
at
the
Brown
show
or
refunded
at
point
of
purchase.
</p>








<p>Digable
fans
do
have
one
thing
to
look
forward
to
in
2013,
though:
one
of
their
albums
will
be
rereleased
with
new
liner
notes.</p>








<p><br
/>
<strong>OK,
so
what
happened
with
the
Digable
show?</strong></p>








<p>Mecca,
in
all
her
royal
inglory,
canceled
the
show
last
week.
First
when
I
got
back
from
[Shabazz
Palaces'
fall
tour]
in
Europe,
I
called
her,
like,
&quot;Yo,
what
day
you
coming
out
here,
so
we
can
rehearse?&quot;
And
she
was
like,
&quot;I
can
only
come
out
on
a
Friday.&quot;
And
the
show's
on
Saturday.
She
said
she
had
a
prior
obligation.
I'm
like,
&quot;Aight.
If
that's
what
it
is,
then
I'll
rehearse
everybody,
and
when
you
get
here,
you
can
just
fall
in
line.
It'll
all
be
gravy.&quot;
She
said,
&quot;Aight.&quot;
The
next
day,
I
called
her
and
she
didn't
call
me
back.
So
then
I
emailed
her
and
was
like,
&quot;What's
going
on?&quot;
She
was
like,
&quot;How
much
is
the
promoter
giving
me?&quot;
'Me,'
like
that.
And
I'm
like,
&quot;You?
The
promoter's
paying
us,
as
a
group.
Do
you
need
an
individual
contract?&quot;
She
never
hit
me
back.
</p>








<p>So
then
I
hit
her,
like
&quot;What's
going
on?
I
need
to
know
what's
happening
so
I
can
take
care
of
you,&quot;
whatever.
So
then
she
calls
and
says,
&quot;I
need
to
see
a
contract.&quot;
So
I'm
like,
cool,
whatever
&#8212;
it's
the
Neptune,
[booked
by
Seattle
Theatre
Group's
well-known
talent
buyer]
Adam
Zacks,
everything's
good!
I
didn't
know
what
she
was
worried
about.
So
I
tracked
everything
down,
sent
her
[a
copy
of]
the
contract.
Two
days
later
she
hits
me
back
and
says,
&quot;Nobody
signed
the
contract.
Is
this
thing
for
real?&quot;
I'm
like,
&quot;Mec,
what
are
you
talking
about?
Check
the
website,
check
ticket
sales,
check
anything.
Just
check
my
resume.
Of
course
it's
real.&quot;
</p>








<p>So
we
tracked
Adam
down
again.
This
is
all
going
through
[our
booking
agent].
So
we
get
[Zacks]
to
sign
it.
He
sends
it
to
her.
She
emails
me
back,
because
she
won't
answer
the
phone,
and
says,
&quot;Hey,
I
need
to
get
$5,000
personally.
My
definitive
rate
is
$5,000.&quot;
The
email
said,
&quot;Am
I
being
punked?
This
is
the
most
unprofessional
deal
I've
ever
been
a
part
of.
My
definitive
rate
is
$5,000
for
myself.
Not
including
expenses
and
travel.
I
can't
do
this.
You
wasted
everybody's
time.&quot;
That's
what
the
email
said.
And
then
she
wouldn't
pick
up
the
phone
or
answer
texts.
So,
I
don't
know
what
the
deal
is.
Maybe
she's
scared
or
doesn't
want
to
come.
I
don't
know
what
she's
doing
for
entertainment,
but
$5,000
a
night
is
unheard
of,
unless
you're
on
the
upper
levels
of
performing.
And
calling
those
guys
unprofessional...it's
all
just
weird.</p>








<p>But
one
time
[in
the
past]
we
had
a
25-city
tour
set
up,
which
she
set
up
&#8212;
made
us
quit
one
booking
agent
and
go
through
someone
that
she
wanted
to
go
with
&#8212;
and
the
day
before
the
tour,
she
quit
the
tour.
That
was
our
last
experience
with
her.
So
this
time
was
kind
of
a
roll
of
the
dice.
But
we
love
this
[music],
and
wanted
to
do
it.</p>








<p>So
that's
what
happened.
And
I
just
wanted
to
let
cats
know
that,
Knowledge
[aka
Cee
Knowledge/Doodlebug]
and
I,
more
than
making
money,
we
just
feel
honored
and
crazy-surprised
and
appreciative
that
fans
want
to
see
us
at
all.
No
amount
of
money,
or
small
amount
of
money
&#8212;
which,
the
money
wasn't
small
at
all,
trust
me
&#8212;
would
keep
us
from
performing
for
people
who
wanted
to
pay
to
see
us
perform.
To
do
[what
Mecca
did]
is
totally
disrespectful
and
a
lack
of
appreciation
for
the
fans
that
make
us
have
the
ability
to
do
the
things
we're
doing
now,
in
the
present.
So
I'm
sorry
for
that.
And
I'm
sorry
that
I
tried
to
do
something,
and
got
some
people
excited
to
come
see
the
show,
and
wasn't
able
to
pull
it
off.
But
I
tried.
And
so
did
Doodlebug.
And
it's
really
through
no
fault
of
our
own.
And
you
know
me,
I
don't
even
like
talking
about
inside
stuff.
But
this
was
a
different
situation,
because
Mecca,
for
whatever
reason,
ruined
it.
And
tried
to
make
it
seem
like
the
deal
was
unprofessional
and
the
money
wasn't
right,
but
I
know
that
the
deal
wasn't
unprofessional
and
I
also
know
that
the
money
was
really
good.
It's
a
heartbreaker.</p>








<p><strong>Sounds
like
she
was
making
those
stipulations
so
that
the
situation
could
be
impossible.</strong></p>








<p>I
can't
speculate.
But
let
me
ask
you
this,
let's
say
your
definitive
rate
was
$5,000.
Wouldn't
that
be
the
first
thing
that
came
out
of
your
mouth?
Why
you
asking
people
to
jump
through
all
these
hoops,
and
at
the
end
of
all
the
hoops,
then
you
pull
everything
with
the
price
bit?</p>








<p><strong>So
would
you
say
Digable's
status
is
indefinite,
or
broken
up?</strong></p>








<p>I
just
can't
see
it.
I
think
it's
the
end.</p>
