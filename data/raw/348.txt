<h3

<div

<div
id="wikiAbstract"
class="wiki-abstract"
data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











Electric
Light
Orchestra
were
a











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/symphonic%20rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">symphonic
rock</a>
band
which
formed
in
Birmingham,
England
in
1970.
The
band
was
formed
by











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jeff+Lynne"
class="bbcode_artist">Jeff
Lynne</a>
(vocals,
guitar,
multiple
instruments),











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Roy+Wood"
class="bbcode_artist">Roy
Wood</a>
(multiple
instruments,
vocals),











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Richard+Tandy"
class="bbcode_artist">Richard
Tandy</a>
(keyboards,
vocals)
and











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bev+Bevan"
class="bbcode_artist">Bev
Bevan</a>
(drums).
Lynne,
Wood
and
Bevan
were
former
members
of
the
psychedelic
rock
band











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Move"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Move</a>.
The
band’s
lineup
would
fluctuate
throughout
its
original
incarnation,
although
Lynne,
Tandy
and
Bevan
would
remain
constant
members.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
They
incorporated
the
sounds
of
string
ensembles,
vocoders
and
dub
echos
into
rock
songs,
thereby
creating
a
very
sophisticated
studio
version
of
rock.
The
musical
content
of
ELO
songs
often
went
far
beyond
usual
chord
structures,
mixing
pop
songwriting
with
classical
romanticism
and
synthesized
sounds.
The
band
claim
that
their
music
“picks
up
where
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Beatles</a>’
1967
song
<a
title="The
Beatles
–
I
Am
the
Walrus"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles/_/I+Am+the+Walrus"
class="bbcode_track">I
Am
the
Walrus</a>
left
off.”<br
/>
<br
/>
Formed
in
1970
by
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Roy+Wood"
class="bbcode_artist">Roy
Wood</a>,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jeff+Lynne"
class="bbcode_artist">Jeff
Lynne</a>
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bev+Bevan"
class="bbcode_artist">Bev
Bevan</a>
(the
remaining
members
of
the
1960s
rock
group
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Move"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Move</a>).
The
band
used
cellos,
violin,
horns
and
woodwinds
to
give
their
music
a
classical
sound.
This
was
an
idea
Roy
Wood
initially
had
while
with
The
Move,
to
take
rock
music
in
a
new
direction.
In
1970
when
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Carl+Wayne"
class="bbcode_artist">Carl
Wayne</a>
left
the
The
Move,
Jeff
Lynne,
front
man
with
fellow
Brum
band
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Idle+Race"
class="bbcode_artist">Idle
Race</a>,
responded
to
Wood’s
second
invitation
to
join
the
line-up,
with
the
lure
of
starting
the
new
band.
</span>
