<h2
class="rm">Biography</h2>










<h3
class="remove-top-margin">Stillwater,

</hgroup>








</header>








<div
class="wiki">









<div
id="wikiAbstract"
class="wiki-abstract"
data-require="music/wiki">










<div
class="wiki-text">











The
All-American
Rejects
are
a











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/rock"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">rock</a>
band
that
formed
in
Stillwater,
Oklahoma,
United
States
in
the
late
1990s
by
high
school
friends
Tyson
Ritter
(on
vocals,
bass,
piano)
and
Nick
Wheeler
(lead
guitar,
vocals
and
keys).
Other
current
members
are
Chris
Gaylor
(on
drums
and
percussion)
and
Mike
Kennerty
(rhythm
guitar
and
vocals).
They
released
their
first
independent
album
in
the
spring
of
2000,
and
eventually
built
up
a
large
local
fanbase.











<span
class="hide">
<br
/><br
/>
In
the
summer
of
2001,
the
Rejects
released
the
EP
<a
title="All-American
Rejects
-
Same
Girl,
New
Songs"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/All-American+Rejects/Same+Girl,+New+Songs"
class="bbcode_album">Same
Girl,
New
Songs</a>
and
began
to
tour
the
American
Midwest.
By
the
tour’s
end,
the
band
had
signed
a
contract
with
Doghouse
Records,
the
label
of
such
bands
as
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/River+City+High"
class="bbcode_artist">River
City
High</a>
and
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+Get+Up+Kids"
class="bbcode_artist">The
Get
Up
Kids</a>.
<br
/>
<br
/>
In
2002
the
Rejects
recorded
their
debut
album.
From
this
album
came
the
single
<a
title="All-American
Rejects
–
Swing,
Swing"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/All-American+Rejects/_/Swing,+Swing"
class="bbcode_track">Swing,
Swing</a>
for
which
the
Rejects
were
first
known.
It
got
them
a
deal
with
DreamWorks,
who
picked
up
the
album
from
Doghouse
Records.
Speaking
with
<a
href="http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=25896"
rel="nofollow">Songfacts</a>
in
a
2012
interview,
Nick
Wheeler
recalled:
“We
knew
that
was
special,
but
we
were
on
a
little
label
that
didn’t
really
talk
to
radio
or
MTV,
so
there
really
wasn’t
talk
of
a
single.
Turns
out
it
turned
the
heads
of
a
lot
of
publishers
and
label
people.”<br
/>
<br
/>
In
2005,
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/The+All-American+Rejects"
class="bbcode_artist">The
All-American
Rejects</a>
released
their
new
album,
<a
title="All-American
Rejects
-
Move
Along"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/All-American+Rejects/Move+Along"
class="bbcode_album">Move
Along</a>.
This
album
features
the
song
<a
title="All-American
Rejects
–
Dirty
Little
Secret"
href="http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/All-American+Rejects/_/Dirty+Little+Secret"
class="bbcode_track">Dirty
Little
Secret</a>,
which
was
the
first
single.
Their
music
video
for
the
song
features
postcards
from
the
book
“Post
Secret”
by
Frank
Warren.
</span>
