<div

Gary
Allan
Herzberg
(born
December
5,
1967)
is
an
American











<a
href="http://www.last.fm/tag/country"
class="bbcode_tag"
rel="tag">country</a>
music
artist,
known
professionally
as
Gary
Allan.











<br
/>











<br
/>
Allan
was
born
and
raised
in
La
Mirada,
California,
to
Harley
and
Mary
Herzberg.
To
ensure
that
the
family
would
focus
on
music,
Allan’s
mother
insisted
that
the
family’s
guitars
would
always
remain
visible
in
the
home.
At
age
thirteen,
Allan
began
playing
in
honky
tonks
with
his
father.











<span
class="hide">
Two
years
later,
he
was
offered
his
first
recording
contract,
from
<span
title="Unknown
label"
class="bbcode_unknown">A&amp;M
Records</span>,
but
rejected
the
deal.
His
parents
wanted
him
to
finish
his
education
and
his
father
felt
that
Allan
had
yet
to
develop
his
own
distinctive
style.
Despite
his
commitment
to
finishing
school,
Allan
reflects
that
he
was
rarely
alert
in
class.
“I
played
the
bars
at
night,
I
was
half
asleep
when
I
got
to
school.
I
thought
sleep
was
what
you
did
when
you
got
to
school.”
After
finishing
school,
he
continued
to
play
in
the
bars
with
his
band,
the
<span
title="Unknown
artist"
class="bbcode_unknown">Honky
Tonk
Wranglers</span>.
Many
of
the
venues
they
played
were
packed,
and
promoters
often
tried
to
move
them
to
larger
clubs.
The
moves
would
have
required
him
to
stop
playing
some
of
the
older
country
music,
such
as
covers
of
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/George+Jones"
class="bbcode_artist">George
Jones</a>
songs,
so
Allan
refused.<br
/>
<br
/>
Allan
was
introduced
to
songwriter/producer
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Byron+Hill"
class="bbcode_artist">Byron
Hill</a>
on
August
28,
1993
by
a
mutual
friend
and
talent-scout
<a
href="http://www.last.fm/music/Jim+seal"
class="bbcode_artist">Jim
seal</a>
at
a
bar
called
the
Lion
D’or
in
Downey,
California,
where
Allan
was
already
regularly
performing.
</span>
