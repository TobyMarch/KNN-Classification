<h2><span
class="mw-headline"
id="B.O.N.E_Enterprise_early_years">B.O.N.E
Enterprise
early
years</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1"
title="Edit
section:
B.O.N.E
Enterprise
early
years">edit</a>]</span></h2>








<p>Formed
in
the
early
1990s,
the
group
was
originally
called
&quot;The
Band-Aid
Boys&quot;.
The
group
formed
the
band
B.O.N.E.
Enterpri$e
which
consisted
of
five
members:
<a
href="/wiki/Krayzie_Bone?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Krayzie
Bone">Krayzie
Bone</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/Layzie_Bone?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Layzie
Bone">Layzie
Bone</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/Bizzy_Bone?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Bizzy
Bone">Bizzy
Bone</a>,
<a
href="/wiki/Wish_Bone?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Wish
Bone">Wish
Bone</a>
and
later
<a
href="/wiki/Flesh-n-Bone?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"

and
recorded
an
album
entitled
<i><a
href="/wiki/Faces_of_Death_(album)?qsrc=3044"

of
Death
(album)">Faces
of
Death</a></i>

of

then

Kermit
Henderson
(Krayzie
Bone's
younger

on

label

Burke
in

Like

many
aspiring
rappers
around
the

they
put
in
calls
to
executives
at
record
companies,
hoping
to
find
someone
who
would
listen.
In

of

a

the

was
given
an
audition
over
the
phone
receiving

<a
href="/wiki/Eazy-E?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>
to

Determined

the

Angeles.

for

Greyhound
trek

months
on
the
city
streets,

in
frequent
calls
to
find
him.
Nothing
came
of
except

that
Eazy-E
was,
in
fact,
on
his
way
to
Cleveland
for
a
show.
Diego
Blak
(born
Diego
Hodge),
a

and

and
co-executive
producer
of
Faces
Of
Death
introduced

to
Eazy-E
at

in

Ohio

for

California

to

On

2,

auditioned
backstage
for
Eazy-E.
Krayzie
performed
his
verse
of
&quot;Flow
Motion&quot;
from
the
Faces
of
Death
album
and
Eazy
was
impressed.<sup
id="cite_ref-4"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>
At
this
point
Eazy
named
them
Thugs-n-Harmony,
but

wanted
to
keep
the
bone
name
so

renamed
themselves
Bone
Thugs-N-Harmony.
Eazy
signed
the
group
(minus
Flesh-n-Bone)
to
his
label
<a
href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Ruthless
Records">Ruthless
Records</a>.<sup

class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<a

<a

R&amp;B/Hip-Hop

Bone</a>&quot;

<h3><span

<p>In

<i>E.

<h2><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Style_and_technique">Style
and
technique</span>
<span
class="mw-editsection">[<a
href="/wiki?title=Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15"
title="Edit
section:
Style
and
technique">edit</a>]</span></h2>








<p>Bone's
style
can
be
described
as
melodic,
yet
rapid-fire
and
gangsta.
Their
flow
is
often
interwoven
at
a
high-speed
<a
href="/wiki/Chopper_(rap)?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Chopper
(rap)">Chopper</a>
style,
melodic
pace
or
slow,
harmonic
pace.
Although
largely
uncredited,
their
monotonous
style
of
singing
as
well
as
their
rapid
fire
rhythms
over
slowed
down
beats
has
influenced
and
pioneered
the
double
time
rhythms
in
R'n'B
that
emerged
from
the
mid-90s
onward,
as
showcased
in
songs
such
&quot;My
love
is
the
Shhhh&quot;
by
Something
for
the
People,
&quot;Say
My
Name&quot;
by
Destiny's
Child,
and
others.
They
also
tend
to
ride
the
beat
that
they're
rapping
over.
They
often
describe
their
style
as
the
&quot;flip
flop
flow&quot;.
Sometimes
there
are
very
few
choruses
separating
their
verses
from
one
another.
In
the
beginning,
circa
Faces
of
Death,
Bone
used
a
pseudo-<a
href="/wiki/Jamaican_patois?qsrc=3044"
onmousedown="triggerActionLog('primaryContent','','center','click','primaryContent','','');"
title="Jamaican
patois"
class="mw-redirect">Jamaican
patois</a>
delivery
with
their
trademark
style,
though
they
rarely
do
this
now.
Bone's
subject
matter
has
both
spiritual
and
occult
undertones
(e.g.,
&quot;Hell
Sent&quot;,
&quot;Intro&quot;,
&quot;Mr.
Ouija&quot;,
&quot;Mr.
Ouija
2&quot;
and
&quot;Da
Introduction&quot;).
At
first,
their
subject
matter
was
almost
always
exclusively
about
violence,
death
and
smoking
marijuana.
Today,
however,
their
subject
matter
includes
other
topics
such
as
urban
socio-political
issues,
positive
messages,
their
old
friend
and
mentor
Eazy-E
and
religion.
Besides
minor
changes
to
subject
matter
and
sound,
their
lyrical
style,
elocution,
and
overall
methodology
remain
intact
and
have
influenced
many
new
artists
today
who
have
picked
up
this
unique
style.</p>
